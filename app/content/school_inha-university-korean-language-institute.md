## School Overview
Inha University, originally founded in 1954 with the support of Korean-American immigrants in Hawaii and President Syngman Rhee, is a highly prestigious private research university. Located in the vibrant, historical port city of Incheon, Inha University's Korean Language Institute (KLI) provides top-tier Korean language education to thousands of international students from around the globe.

The institute focuses on comprehensive language training—speaking, listening, reading, and writing—while helping students integrate seamlessly into Korean university life and society.

## Curriculum & Program Structure
The KLI offers a highly structured, 6-level systematic curriculum aligned with the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) and the Test of Proficiency in Korean (TOPIK).
- **Duration**: 10 weeks per semester (200 hours total per term)
- **Schedule**: Monday to Friday, 4 hours a day (typically 09:00 - 13:00 or 13:30 - 17:30)
- **Class Size**: Average of 12–15 students per class to ensure personalized attention.
- **Levels 1-2 (Beginner)**: Focuses on basic survival Korean, daily conversations, and understanding essential Korean customs.
- **Levels 3-4 (Intermediate)**: Focuses on expressive language skills, vocabulary expansion, social interactions, and understanding fundamental Korean social/cultural issues.
- **Levels 5-6 (Advanced)**: Focuses on academic Korean, discussion skills, professional writing, and deep analysis of Korean politics, economy, and literature.

## Campus Facilities & Accommodation
Students enrolled in the Inha Korean Language Institute have access to state-of-the-art university facilities:
- **On-Campus Dormitories**: Inha University prioritizes international KLI students for dormitory placement. Rooms are typically shared (double or quadruple occupancy) and fully equipped with high-speed Wi-Fi, laundry facilities, study lounges, and gym access.
- **Inha University Library**: KLI students receive a student ID card giving them full access to the vast multimedia library resources and study rooms.
- **Student Dining**: Multiple on-campus cafeterias serve affordable, nutritious meals, including halal and vegetarian options in designated areas.

## Student Support & Culture Programs
Inha University is deeply committed to student welfare and cultural immersion:
- **Cultural Activities**: Every semester, KLI organizes field trips and cultural hands-on experiences, such as visiting historic palaces, making traditional Korean crafts (hanji/pottery), attending K-pop concerts, and participating in Korean cooking classes.
- **Inha Buddy Program**: KLI students are paired with domestic Inha University undergraduate students for language exchange, helping foreign students adapt quickly to Korean life.
- **Visa & Administrative Assistance**: Dedicated foreign coordinators provide guidance on D-4 visa applications, alien registration card (ARC) issuance, health insurance, and banking setup.

## University Pathways & Scholarships
For students planning to pursue degree programs in South Korea:
- **Direct Path**: Successful completion of Level 4 or higher at Inha KLI satisfies the language requirement for undergraduate admission at Inha University.
- **Scholarship Benefits**: Inha KLI offers academic excellence scholarships (covering 10% to 50% of the next semester's tuition) for top-performing students in each level, as well as special tuition waivers for students progressing directly to Inha University's undergraduate or graduate schools.
