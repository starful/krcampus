# Master Guide to UNIST: The Ultimate Study-Abroad Handbook

Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology (UNIST) is one of South Korea's four prestigious public universities dedicated to science and technology. Founded to become a world-leading research institution, UNIST has rapidly established itself as a global powerhouse in engineering and natural sciences. This guide offers an in-depth look at UNIST's academic programs, scholarships, admissions, and student life for international applicants.

---

## 1. University Overview

Located in Ulsan, the industrial heart of South Korea, UNIST bridges academic research and industrial application. Since its establishment in 2007, UNIST has grown rapidly, earning global recognition for high-impact research.

*   **Address:** 50 UNIST-gil, Eonyang-eup, Ulju-gun, Ulsan, 44919, Republic of Korea
*   **Acceptance Rate:** 15% - 20%
*   **International Student Body:** Over 550 international students.

UNIST ranks among the world's top young universities, with a stellar reputation in Materials Science and Chemical Engineering. Its location in Ulsan—home to industrial giants like Hyundai and SK Energy—creates an ideal ecosystem for research partnerships, internships, and post-graduation employment.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

A defining feature of UNIST is its global academic environment. **100% of lectures are in English** for both undergraduate and graduate levels.

Key international support features include:
*   **Dual Degree & Exchange Programs:** Partnerships with elite global universities.
*   **Global Research Internships:** Hands-on experience in state-of-the-art labs.
*   **Language Support:** Free Korean language and culture classes to help students adjust.

## 4. Tuition, Fees & Scholarships

UNIST offers generous financial support. Admitted international students are eligible for a **Full Tuition Waiver** and **Monthly Living Stipends**, subject to maintaining academic standards.

### Scholarship Maintenance Requirements
Undergraduate students must maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 out of 4.3 to retain the tuition waiver. Graduate funding is tied to research (RA) or teaching assistantships (TA), which provide higher monthly allowances.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

Admission to UNIST is highly competitive, seeking outstanding students with a passion for science and innovation.

### Entry Requirements
1.  **Academic Transcripts:** High school diploma (undergraduate) or bachelor's degree (graduate). Strong science/math grades are crucial.
2.  **English Proficiency:** Official test scores are required. Recommended minimums: TOEFL iBT 80 or IELTS 6.0.
3.  **Recommendation Letters:** Required from science or mathematics teachers/professors.
4.  **Statement & Study Plan:** Detailing academic achievements and career goals.

### Application Timeline
*   **Fall Intake (September):** Applications open January – March.
*   **Spring Intake (March):** Applications open August – October.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

Located in Eonyang-eup, Ulsan, the UNIST campus features a peaceful environment centered around Gamak Pond.

### Living on Campus
The university provides high-tech dormitories for international students:
*   Single or double rooms with air conditioning and high-speed internet.
*   On-campus cafeterias (serving Korean and international food), convenience stores, and lounges.
*   A sports complex with a gym, swimming pool, and athletic fields.

### Exploring Ulsan
Ulsan blends natural beauty with industrial strength. Students can visit Taehwagang National Garden, hike the Yeongnam Alps, or explore the city center. Excellent public transit connects the campus to the Ulsan KTX Station, making travel to Busan (20 mins) or Seoul (2 hours) convenient.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I apply to UNIST if I do not speak any Korean?
Yes. All lectures, exams, and resources are in English. While basic Korean helps with daily life off-campus, it is not required for admission or graduation.

### Does the full tuition waiver cover the entire duration of my degree?
Yes. The waiver covers the standard duration (4 years for Bachelor's, 2 years for Master's, 4 years for Ph.D.) if you maintain the minimum GPA (usually 2.0/4.3).

### Are international students allowed to work part-time in Korea?
Yes. On a D-2 visa, you can work part-time (up to 20 hours/week during semesters, unlimited during vacations) with permission from the university and immigration.

### How are the monthly stipends paid out?
Stipends are deposited monthly into your Korean bank account. Amounts depend on your academic level and lab research participation.

### What career support does UNIST provide for finding jobs in Korea?
The Career Development Center offers resume workshops, mock interviews, and job fairs. UNIST's close ties to Ulsan's industrial sector provide excellent networking opportunities with major tech firms.
