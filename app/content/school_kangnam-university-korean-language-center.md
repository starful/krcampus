## 1. School Overview

Kangnam University Korean Language Center, located in Yongin-si, Gyeonggi-do, is a specialized training institution accommodating up to 300 international students. The university is widely recognized for its supportive, inclusive campus culture and excellent student care services. 

Situated just south of Seoul, the campus provides an ideal balance of a focused, affordable academic environment and convenient metropolitan transit access via the Everline and Bundang subway lines. Key structural highlights include guaranteed on-campus housing, a robust university pathway program, and a dedicated buddy mentoring system that pairs international students with Korean peers to accelerate language acquisition and local cultural adaptation.

## 4. Admissions & D-4 Visa Steps

To study at the center for more than 90 days, international applicants must obtain a D-4 (General Student) visa.

1. **Application Submission**: Submit the online application with high school/university graduation certificates, academic transcripts, passport copy, and financial verification documents.
2. **Document Screening**: The university reviews credentials. An online interview may be requested.
3. **Invoice & Tuition Payment**: Once approved, pay the tuition (typically 2 semesters) and application fees to the designated university bank account.
4. **Admission Certificate (CoA)**: Upon confirming payment, the center issues the official Certificate of Admission.
5. **Visa Application**: Apply for the D-4 visa at your local Korean Embassy by submitting the CoA, financial statements showing at least $10,000 USD (or equivalent), and required consular forms.

## 5. TOPIK & University Pathway

Kangnam University actively supports academic progression. The Language Center integrates TOPIK (Test of Proficiency in Korean) preparation courses directly into its regular curriculum to help students qualify for degree programs. 

International students who complete Level 3 or higher are eligible for direct admission to undergraduate programs. Benefits include simplified screening procedures and substantial tuition waiver scholarships (ranging from 30% to over 50% reduction for the first semester), determined by the student's TOPIK level. This makes it a popular starting point for pursuing a full degree in Korea.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I work part-time while studying on a D-4 visa?
Yes. After 6 months (180 days) on a D-4 visa, you can work up to 20 hours per week. You must have at least 90% attendance, a school recommendation, and a basic level of TOPIK proficiency to receive immigration clearance.

### How does the Buddy/Mentoring Program work?
The university matches language students with local Korean student volunteers. This program facilitates natural language practice, assists with adjustment to life in Korea, and includes structured cultural outings.

### What is the minimum attendance requirement?
You must maintain at least an 80% attendance rate per semester. Falling below this threshold will result in academic failure and may cause immigration authorities to revoke or deny your D-4 visa extension.

### Are dormitory rooms single or shared?
Dormitory rooms allocated to language students are primarily shared (double or quad occupancy) to ensure affordability. Shared housing helps students integrate quickly and practice Korean daily.

### Does the center organize cultural experiences?
Yes. Each term features cultural excursions. Activities include traditional pottery workshops, K-Pop dance classes, visits to nearby Everland, and excursions to historic palaces in Seoul.
