# Chung-Ang University (CAU) International Student Guide

Chung-Ang University (중앙대학교) is a leading private university in Seoul, South Korea. Globally recognized for its creative arts, business, and engineering programs, CAU is a dynamic hub for international students seeking a world-class education in an innovative city.

---

## 1. University Overview

Established in 1916, Chung-Ang University (CAU) has evolved into a premier comprehensive institution. Renowned for its creative arts and strong business and science sectors, CAU hosts approximately **4,200 international students** from over 100 countries. With an **estimated acceptance rate of 15% to 25%**, CAU is both competitive and accessible. The university operates two campuses: the main Seoul Campus in Heukseok-dong and the Da Vinci Campus in Anseong, though most international programs are based in Seoul.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

CAU offers robust English-taught tracks for students who do not speak fluent Korean:

*   **Global Business Administration (GBA):** A fully English-taught undergraduate track in the AACSB-accredited Business School, focusing on global trade, finance, and management.
*   **Graduate School of International Studies (GSIS):** Offers master's and doctoral programs entirely in English, specializing in International Trade, Finance, and East Asian Studies.
*   **English-Friendly Majors:** Several departments within the Colleges of Engineering, ICT Engineering, and Natural Sciences offer 30% to 50% of their courses in English.
*   **Exchange Programs:** Active partnerships with over 600 universities globally facilitate study abroad semesters and dual-degree pathways.

---

## 3. Faculties & Academic Strengths

CAU's academic infrastructure is highly specialized. Its **Department of Theater, Film, and Media Studies** is widely ranked as the top program in Korea, producing legendary Hallyu stars. Its **Business and Economics** faculty holds prestigious AACSB accreditation, placing it in the top 5% globally. CAU also excels in future-focused fields like AI, software development, and pharmacy.

### International Scholarships

CAU offers the **CAU Global Scholarship** to support incoming international freshmen:
*   **TOPIK Level 6:** 100% tuition waiver for the first semester.
*   **TOPIK Level 5:** 50% tuition waiver for the first semester.
*   **English Track (IELTS 7.0 / TOEFL iBT 95):** 50% tuition waiver for the first semester.
*   **Academic Excellence:** Up to 100% tuition waiver for subsequent semesters by maintaining a GPA of 3.5 to 4.0.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

Applying to CAU requires careful preparation and timely documentation.

### Basic Admission Checklist
1.  **Academic Records:** High school graduation certificate (undergraduate) or bachelor’s degree (graduate).
2.  **Language Proficiency:**
    *   *Korean Track:* TOPIK Level 3 or higher (Level 4 required for graduation).
    *   *English Track:* TOEFL iBT 80 or IELTS 5.5 or higher.
3.  **Financial Solvency:** A bank balance certificate showing at least $20,000 USD held for 30+ days.
4.  **Consular Legalization:** Graduation certificates and transcripts must be Apostilled or verified by the Korean consulate in your home country.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

CAU’s main campus is located at **84 Heukseok-ro, Dongjak-gu, Seoul**, right along the Han River.

*   **Connectivity:** Served by **Heukseok Station (Line 9)**, offering direct access to **Yeouido** (financial hub) in 10 minutes and **Gangnam** (business district) in under 20 minutes.
*   **Housing:** International students are housed in **Blue Mir Hall**, a modern dormitory complex featuring double rooms, gym facilities, study halls, and convenience stores.
*   **K-Culture Hub:** With its top arts programs, the campus is alive with student-led plays, indie film screenings, and musical performances.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I apply to CAU if I do not speak any Korean?
Yes. You can apply directly to English-taught tracks like the Global Business Administration (GBA) program or enroll in CAU's intensive Korean Language Institute (KLI) for 6–12 months before starting your major.

### What is the estimated living cost for a student at CAU?
Expect to spend 1,200,000 to 1,500,000 KRW per month. This covers on-campus housing (approx. 450,000 KRW/month), meals, transit, health insurance, and personal expenses.

### Are international students permitted to work part-time?
Yes. International students on a D-2 visa can work up to 20 hours per week during semesters (and unlimited hours during breaks). You need approval from the university and immigration, which typically requires TOPIK Level 3 or higher.

### Does CAU offer exchange programs with top Western universities?
Yes. CAU has active credit-transfer agreements with over 600 institutions worldwide, allowing students to study abroad for one or two semesters.

### How competitive is the admission process for the Film and Theater program?
It is extremely competitive, with domestic acceptance rates below 5%. International applicants are evaluated separately but must submit a strong portfolio, show high Korean proficiency (TOPIK 5+ recommended), and pass talent evaluations.
