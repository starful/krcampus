## 1. School Overview

The Korean Language Center (KLC) at Pukyong National University (PKNU) is a state-run language training institution located at **45 Yongso-ro, Nam-gu, Busan, 48513, Republic of Korea**. Situated on a flat, accessible campus near Gwangalli Beach and the Kyungsung University district, PKNU hosts up to **800 students**. The center provides guaranteed housing, buddy tutoring programs, and comprehensive TOPIK support.

---

## 2. Programs & Schedule

PKNU offers structured Regular, Short-term, and Test Preparation courses.

* **Regular Courses (Levels 1 to 6):** 10 weeks per term (200 hours). Classes meet 5 days a week (Mon–Fri) for 4 hours daily. Students are assigned to Morning or Afternoon classes via placement tests.
* **Short-term Programs:** 3-week intensive summer sessions focusing on conversational practice and culture.
* **TOPIK Prep Classes:** Evening/weekend sessions targeting exam strategies.

## 4. Admissions & D-4 Visa Steps

International students studying for more than 90 days must apply for a **D-4-1 (General Student) Visa**.

### Step-by-Step Application Process

1. **Online Application:** Submit the application form and scanned documents.
2. **Screening:** Document review takes approximately 2 to 3 weeks.
3. **Tuition Payment:** Upon acceptance, pay tuition (minimum of two terms, 2,400,000 KRW, plus the 60,000 KRW application fee).
4. **Standard Admission Letter:** PKNU mails the physical Certificate of Admission (COA).
5. **Visa Application:** Submit the COA, passport, and financial proofs to the local Korean Embassy.

### Required Documents
* Complete Application Form and passport-sized photo.
* Passport copy (valid for at least 1 year).
* **Apostilled or Consular-verified** Graduation/Enrollment Certificate.
* **Financial Proof:** Bank statement showing at least **$10,000 USD** held for over one month.
* Family Relationship Certificate (required if using a parent's bank statement).

## 5. TOPIK & University Pathway

PKNU offers pathways for students transitioning from language training to degree programs:

* **TOPIK Preparation:** Special courses are offered twice yearly (January/July, 300,000 KRW) focusing on mock exams and writing.
* **Undergraduate Pathways:** Completing Level 3 or higher waives the official language test requirement for admission into most undergraduate majors.
* **Admission Benefits:** KLC graduates entering PKNU degree programs receive application fee waivers and priority for scholarships.

## 6. Dormitory & Living in the City

### Guaranteed On-Campus Housing
PKNU guarantees dormitory placement for newly registered language students during their first two terms. 
* **Facilities:** Double-occupancy rooms with desks, beds, wardrobes, AC, and Wi-Fi.
* **Amenities:** Card-key security, coin laundry, fitness center, study lounge, and cafeteria.
* **Cost:** Approx. **1,000,000 to 1,300,000 KRW** per 10-week term.

### Living in Nam-gu, Busan
PKNU features a flat campus in student-centric Nam-gu, surrounded by affordable dining, cafes, and transport. The campus is served directly by **Kyungsung University - Pukyong National University Station (Busan Line 2)**, placing Gwangalli Beach 10 minutes away and Haeundae Beach 20 minutes away.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I choose my class shift (Morning or Afternoon)?
Assignments are based on placement test scores. Levels 1-2 often run in the afternoon, while advanced levels run in the morning. Shift requests are reviewed on a case-by-case basis.

### Is the dormitory guaranteed for my entire stay?
Dormitory housing is guaranteed for the first two terms. For subsequent terms, students must re-apply. Selection is based on attendance, GPA, and demerit history.

### How does the Peer-Tutoring (Buddy Program) work?
PKNU pairs international language students with Korean undergraduates to practice conversation, navigate Busan, and participate in social events.

### Am I allowed to work part-time while studying?
Under D-4 visa rules, you can apply for part-time work after 6 months (2 terms) of study if you maintain a 90%+ attendance rate and a minimum GPA of C (2.0).

### What happens if my D-4 visa application is rejected?
Submit your official visa rejection letter to PKNU for a full tuition refund, excluding the non-refundable 60,000 KRW application fee.
