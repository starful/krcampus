# Kyungpook National University (KNU): The Ultimate International Student Guide

## 1. University Overview
Located in the metropolitan city of Daegu, **Kyungpook National University (KNU)** is one of South Korea's premier Flagship Korean National Universities. Established in 1946, KNU has cultivated a stellar reputation for research excellence, academic innovation, and regional economic partnership.

With an active international student community of over **2,200 scholars** from 80+ countries and a selective yet achievable acceptance rate of **55% - 65%**, KNU offers an inclusive global learning environment. The university bridges the gap between high-quality education and economic accessibility. As a flagship public institution, it is funded heavily by the government, ensuring state-of-the-art campus facilities and highly affordable educational resources for ambitious global minds.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs
While Korean is the primary language of instruction, KNU has expanded its English-medium offerings to accommodate international talents.

* **Undergraduate Options:** Selected tracks in Business Administration, Global Software, and Engineering offer courses taught entirely in English.
* **Graduate School:** The Graduate School of International Studies (GSIS) and major STEM departments offer extensive English-taught Master’s and Ph.D. degrees.
* **Global Exchange Pathways:** KNU maintains partnerships with hundreds of universities worldwide, facilitating reciprocal exchange semesters, dual-degree tracks, and summer school initiatives.
* **Language Center:** KNU’s Language Education Center provides structured 10-week intensive Korean courses for those transitioning into Korean-taught majors.

## 4. Tuition, Fees & Scholarships
KNU is highly affordable compared to private universities in Seoul. The yearly tuition fee averages **4,500,000 KRW**, and KNU charges **0 KRW in international undergraduate admission fees**.

| Fee Component | KNU (National) | Seoul Private Univ. (Est.) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Admission Fee** | 0 KRW | 150,000 - 500,000 KRW |
| **Yearly Tuition (IT/Eng)** | ~4,800,000 KRW | ~9,000,000 - 11,000,000 KRW |
| **Yearly Tuition (Biz)** | ~3,600,000 KRW | ~7,500,000 - 9,000,000 KRW |
| **Dormitory (Per Semester)** | ~800,000 - 1,200,000 KRW | ~1,500,000 - 2,500,000 KRW |

### International Scholarships
* **KNU Honors Scholarship:** Offers 100% or 50% tuition waivers for incoming freshmen based on language proficiency test scores (TOPIK Level 3-6 or English equivalents like IELTS/TOEFL).
* **Academic Excellence Scholarship:** Active students in the top 30% of their cohort receive 30% to 80% tuition waivers for the next semester.
* **TOPIK Achievement Grant:** Cash incentives or partial waivers are awarded to students who achieve high TOPIK scores while enrolled.

## 5. Admissions for International Students
KNU runs two admission cycles: **Spring Semester** (applications in Sep/Oct) and **Fall Semester** (applications in Mar/Apr).

### Eligibility & Checklist
1. **Academic Credentials:** High school graduation (for Bachelor's) or Bachelor's degree (for Graduate studies) with official apostille/consular verification.
2. **Language Requirements:**
   * *Korean Track:* Minimum TOPIK Level 3 (TOPIK Level 4 is usually required for graduation).
   * *English Track:* TOEFL iBT 80, IELTS 5.5, or CEFR B2 equivalent.
3. **Application Dossier:** Includes a personal statement, study plan, academic transcripts, and letters of recommendation.
4. **Financial Solvency:** A bank balance certificate of at least $18,000 USD to secure the D-2 student visa.

## 6. Campus Life & Location
KNU is located at **80 Daehak-ro, Buk-gu, Daegu, 41566**. Daegu is South Korea's fourth-largest metropolitan area, blending historical landmarks with advanced technology. It offers an energetic urban lifestyle with a cost of living roughly 30% lower than Seoul.

The main campus is connected to the city via Daegu's efficient subway and bus lines, with rapid KTX train access to Seoul (just 1.5 hours away). On-campus facilities include modern dorms, extensive libraries, gymnasiums, and global lounges. The surrounding college town ("Daehak-ro") features student-friendly cafes, pubs, and shopping. KNU also runs a "Buddy Program" that pairs international students with domestic mentors to assist with cultural adjustment.

## 7. FAQ

### Is KNU a prestigious university?
Yes. KNU is one of the premier Flagship National Universities in South Korea. It is highly respected for research excellence and ranks exceptionally high for job placement in major technology and engineering corporations.

### What is KNU's international acceptance rate?
The acceptance rate is estimated at 55% - 65%. If you meet the official academic qualifications and language prerequisites, you have a very strong chance of acceptance.

### Are dormitories guaranteed for international students?
KNU prioritizes international students for on-campus housing. Rooms are affordable (800,000 to 1,200,000 KRW per semester) and come equipped with essential amenities, study areas, and meal options.

### Can I work part-time while studying at KNU?
Yes. International students on D-2 visas with sufficient TOPIK scores can work part-time up to 20-30 hours per week during semesters and unlimited hours during breaks, subject to university and immigration clearance.

### Do I need to apply separately for KNU scholarships?
No. All international applicants are automatically screened for the KNU Honors and entry scholarships based on their admission application and language test scores.
