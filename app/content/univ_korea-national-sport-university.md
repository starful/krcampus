## University Overview

Founded in 1977, **Korea National Sport University (KNSU)** is South Korea’s premier public institution dedicated to the advancement of physical education, professional athletics, and sports science. Located in the Songpa-gu district of Seoul—directly adjacent to the historic Seoul Olympic Park—KNSU has established itself as an elite nursery for world-class athletes, coaches, sports scientists, and industry leaders.

KNSU boasts an unparalleled athletic legacy. Its alumni and students have won a significant portion of South Korea's total Olympic medals, making it a legendary name in the global sports community. However, KNSU is not just for elite athletes; it has robust academic tracks in sports medicine, leisure industry management, and special physical education, making it an excellent destination for international students seeking careers in the global sports industry.

## Academic Programs & Structure

While KNSU is highly famous for its practical athletic training, it houses a comprehensive range of academic programs designed to prepare students for diverse roles in the sports sector:

*   **Department of Physical Education:** Focused on nurturing elite athletes, coaches, and physical education teachers.
*   **Department of Sports Medicine:** Highly sought-after program specializing in athletic training, rehabilitation, and sports physiology.
*   **Department of Taekwondo:** Dedicated to preserving and globally propagating Korea's traditional martial art through scientific training and cultural studies.
*   **Department of Sports Industry and Leisure:** Combines sports management, marketing, and recreational studies to prepare students for the corporate sports market.
*   **Department of Special Physical Education:** Specializes in adaptive sports and physical education for individuals with disabilities.

## Admissions & Requirements for International Students

International admissions at KNSU are divided into two main tracks: **Elite Athlete Track** (for active competitors) and the **Academic Track** (for sports science, management, and medicine).

### Language Proficiency
*   **Undergraduate Studies:** Most lectures are conducted in Korean. Applicants generally require a minimum of **TOPIK Level 3** (or Level 4 for certain programs like Sports Medicine).
*   **Graduate School:** Offers more English-friendly research pathways, though a basic command of Korean is highly recommended for campus integration.

### Application Portfolio
For practical sports majors, applicants must submit official athletic records, certificates of competition, or video portfolios demonstrating their physical capabilities. For academic majors, prior academic performance, a detailed study plan, and personal statements carry the most weight.

## Tuition, Living Costs & Scholarships

As a **national public university**, KNSU offers some of the most competitive tuition rates in Seoul. 

*   **Undergraduate Tuition:** Approximately 1.8 to 2.1 million KRW per semester (~3.6 to 4.2 million KRW annually), which is roughly half the cost of private universities in Seoul.
*   **Scholarships:** KNSU offers academic excellence scholarships for international students, which can cover 30% to 100% of tuition fees based on GPA or athletic performance. Additionally, government-funded programs like the Global Korea Scholarship (GKS) are applicable here.

## Campus Life & Facilities

KNSU is equipped with world-class facilities designed for peak athletic performance and research:

*   **Indoor & Outdoor Arenas:** Olympic-sized swimming pools, dedicated gymnastics halls, indoor ice rinks, velodromes, and specialized martial arts training halls.
*   **Sports Science Institute:** Features cutting-edge biomechanics labs, motion capture systems, and metabolic chambers where students can conduct advanced research.
*   **Dormitories:** On-campus housing is available for international students at an affordable rate, complete with nutritious meal plans designed specifically for active individuals.
*   **Location:** Situated in Songpa-gu, students have immediate access to the Olympic Park, Lotte World Tower, and excellent public transportation (Seoul Subway Line 5 and 9) linking them to the rest of Seoul.

## Career Prospects for International Graduates

Graduating from KNSU opens prestigious doors globally. International alumni go on to become:
*   National team coaches and athletic trainers in their home countries.
*   Sports rehabilitation specialists in hospitals, clinics, and professional sports clubs.
*   Sports marketing and event management professionals.
*   Researchers and professors in sports science and biomechanics.
*   International sports administrators working with organizations like the IOC, FIFA, or national Olympic committees.
