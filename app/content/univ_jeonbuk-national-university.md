# Jeonbuk National University (JBNU): Complete International Student Guide

Jeonbuk National University (전북대학교), located in South Korea's historic center, is one of the country's ten Flagship Korean National Universities. Known for top-tier research, a green campus, and low tuition, JBNU is an ideal gateway for global minds seeking a world-class education without Seoul's high costs.

---

## 1. University Overview

Founded in 1947, Jeonbuk National University (JBNU) is the intellectual hub of the Jeollabuk-do province. Funded substantially by the government, JBNU maintains cutting-edge research facilities and highly affordable programs.

The university serves over 32,000 students, including approximately 1,600 international students from 70+ countries. JBNU ranks among the top 10 national universities in South Korea and is globally recognized in agricultural sciences, veterinary medicine, and engineering.

Located in Jeonju, a UNESCO Creative City of Gastronomy and home to the famous Hanok Village, JBNU blends advanced modern learning with authentic Korean culture.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

While Korean is the primary language of instruction, JBNU offers expanding global tracks.

### English-Track Degrees
JBNU offers select undergraduate and many graduate (Master's and Ph.D.) programs entirely in English, primarily in:
*   **College of Engineering** (Smart Semiconductor, Mechanical, and Chemical Engineering)
*   **College of Agriculture and Life Sciences**
*   **Graduate School of International Studies (GSIS)**

### Korean Language Institute (KLI)
For students needing Korean proficiency, the KLI offers intensive preparation:
*   **Duration:** 4 semesters/year (Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter), 10 weeks/semester.
*   **Goal:** Reach TOPIK Level 3 or higher, the baseline for undergraduate degrees.

## 4. Tuition, Fees & Scholarships

JBNU offers competitive tuition rates roughly 50% lower than private universities in Seoul. JBNU has also eliminated the undergraduate admission fee for all enrolled students.

### Annual Tuition Comparison (Estimates in KRW)

| Expense Type | JBNU (Flagship National) | Average Seoul Private University (Est.) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Admission Fee** | 0 KRW | 150,000 - 500,000 KRW |
| **Yearly Tuition (Humanities)** | ~3,200,000 KRW | ~7,000,000 - 8,500,000 KRW |
| **Yearly Tuition (Engineering/Sciences)**| ~4,200,000 KRW | ~9,000,000 - 11,000,000 KRW |
| **Average Annual Tuition** | **~3,800,000 KRW** | **~8,500,000 KRW** |

### Scholarships for International Students
JBNU offers generous scholarships to offset tuition:
*   **New Student TOPIK Scholarship:** TOPIK Level 6 (100% waiver), Level 5 (80%), Level 4 (40%).
*   **English Proficiency Scholarship:** TOEFL iBT 90+ or IELTS 6.5+ yields 40% to 100% waivers.
*   **Academic Excellence Scholarship:** 30% to 100% tuition waivers based on the previous semester's GPA.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

JBNU has an estimated international student acceptance rate of 45-55%, depending on the major and language readiness.

### Admission Requirements
1.  **Academics:** 12 years of primary/secondary education for Undergraduate; a Bachelor's degree for Graduate.
2.  **Language Proficiency:**
    *   *Korean Track:* Minimum TOPIK Level 3 (Medicine, Nursing, Vet Medicine require Level 5 or 6).
    *   *English Track:* TOEFL iBT 80, IELTS 5.5, or CEFR B2.
3.  **Financials:** Bank statement showing $16,000 - $18,000 USD held for over a month.

### Application Timeline
*   **Spring Intake (Starts March):** Apply September to November.
*   **Fall Intake (Starts September):** Apply March to May.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

### The Jeonju Experience
Jeonju is Korea’s cultural capital, home to the famous Hanok Village and the birthplace of *Bibimbap*. The cost of living is 30-40% lower than Seoul, allowing students to maintain a high quality of life affordably.

### On-Campus Facilities
*   **Architecture:** Traditional Hanok gate design paired with modern academic facilities.
*   **Housing:** Safe on-campus dorms (double occupancy) with gym, study halls, and internet. Semesters cost 1,000,000 to 1,500,000 KRW (with optional meal plans).
*   **Global Lounge:** A dedicated campus hub for support, language exchange, and activities.

## 7. FAQ

### Is it possible to study at JBNU if I do not speak any Korean?
Yes. You can enroll in English-taught programs at the graduate level or select undergraduate engineering/business tracks. Alternatively, you can study at the KLI to achieve TOPIK Level 3 before starting your degree.

### What is the estimated monthly cost of living in Jeonju?
An international student needs about 700,000 to 900,000 KRW (approx. $520 - $680 USD) monthly. This covers dormitory/shared housing, meals, transport, and personal expenses.

### Are international students permitted to work part-time?
Yes. With a D-2 visa and university approval, you can work up to 20 hours per week during semesters, and unlimited hours during breaks.

### How competitive is the admission process?
The international acceptance rate is 45-55%. Meeting academic standards and holding a strong TOPIK or English certificate highly increases your chances.

### How do I travel to Jeonju from Incheon International Airport?
Take a direct intercity limousine bus from Incheon Airport to Jeonju (approx. 3 to 3.5 hours) or take the high-speed KTX train from Seoul Station.
