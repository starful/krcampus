# Guide to Soongsil University Korean Language Institute

## 1. School Overview

The Soongsil University Korean Language Institute (숭실대학교 한국어교육원), located at **369 Sangdo-ro, Dongjak-gu, Seoul, 06978, Republic of Korea**, has a student capacity of **800 students**. It offers a focused, personalized learning environment for international students. Known for technological innovation and a global community, the institute serves as a reliable gateway to undergraduate or graduate programs in Korea. Key highlights include modern on-campus housing, a 1:1 Korean Buddy Program, intensive TOPIK prep courses, and systematic admissions counseling.

---

## 2. Programs & Schedule

Soongsil University operates its regular Korean curriculum across four quarterly semesters: Spring, Summer, Fall, and Winter.

### Curriculum Structure
* **Levels:** Level 1 (Beginner) to Level 6 (Advanced).
* **Duration:** 10 weeks per semester (200 hours total, 4 hours a day, Monday to Friday).
* **Shifts:** Morning session (09:00 - 13:00) or Afternoon session (13:30 - 17:30) depending on placement.

## 4. Admissions & D-4 Visa Steps

Students intending to study for two semesters (6 months) or longer must obtain a D-4-1 (General Student) visa.

### Step 1: Document Preparation
Prepare the following standard application package:
1. Completed application form and passport-sized photo.
2. Photocopy of passport personal information page.
3. Apostilled or consular-verified high school graduation (or higher) certificate.
4. Bank balance certificate showing a minimum of $10,000 USD held for over 30 days.
5. Family relation certificate (if the bank balance is under a parent's name).

### Step 2: Online Application & Review
Submit the digital dossier to the Soongsil Language Institute portal. Admissions reviews documents for academic authenticity and financial viability.

### Step 3: Fee Payment
Upon approval, pay the tuition invoice (typically 2 to 4 semesters, depending on nationality) and application fee via bank wire to the designated virtual account.

### Step 4: Certificate of Admission (CoA) Issuance
Once payment is received, Soongsil dispatches the physical Certificate of Admission (CoA) and university business registration certificate.

### Step 5: Visa Application & Arrival
Apply for the D-4 visa at the nearest Korean Embassy or Consulate. Once approved, book your flight, complete the online placement test, and arrive for student orientation.

## 5. TOPIK & University Pathway

Soongsil provides systematic pathways to undergraduate and graduate programs:

* **TOPIK Prep Classes:** Target-driven TOPIK preparation courses focusing on test strategies, TOPIK II essay writing, and mock exams.
* **Admission Counseling:** 1:1 counseling sessions covering application reviews, essay coaching, and interview prep for universities in Seoul.
* **Pathways & Scholarships:** Completing Level 3 or 4 at the institute grants language proficiency admission waivers and pathway scholarships, reducing initial university tuition.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I work part-time while studying on a D-4 visa?
Yes, after completing six months of continuous studies with a 90%+ attendance rate. Eligible students can work up to 20 hours per week during semesters with immigration approval.

### Is the on-campus dormitory guaranteed for all language students?
No. Rooms are assigned first-come, first-served. Priority is given to newly admitted overseas students who apply and pay immediately upon receipt of their invoice.

### How does the Korean Buddy Program work?
The institute pairs international students 1:1 with domestic Soongsil undergraduate students for conversation practice, cultural exchange, and daily life help in Seoul.

### What happens if my attendance drops below 80%?
Korean immigration law requires at least 80% attendance per semester to pass and extend a D-4 visa. Falling below this threshold results in visa renewal denial and mandatory departure.

### Can I get a tuition refund if my visa is rejected?
Yes. If your visa is officially denied by the Embassy, submit a refund request with the rejection notice. The institute refunds tuition, excluding the non-refundable registration fee.
