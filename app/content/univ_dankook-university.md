# Dankook University (DKU) International Student Guide

Dankook University (DKU), founded in 1947 as Korea's first post-liberation private university, is a premier institution hosting over 2,200 international students. Operating a specialized dual-campus system with a **40%** acceptance rate, DKU offers competitive yet accessible global education. This guide covers academics, costs, admissions, and campus life.

---

## 1. University Overview

DKU's innovative **dual-campus system** optimizes academic focus:
* **Jukjeon Campus (Yongin):** Specializes in IT, Software, Humanities, and Social Sciences. Located near Gyeonggi-do's tech valley, it offers close links to tech giants.
* **Cheonan Campus (Cheonan):** Focuses on Biotechnology (BT), Medicine, Dentistry, and Agriculture, fostering a major biomedical cluster.

Its strategic location in the Seoul Metropolitan Area grants students excellent internship and employment options with global corporations.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

DKU offers English-taught tracks and support programs for international students.

### Key English-Taught Undergraduate Programs
* **Mobile System Engineering (MSE):** Focuses on next-gen communication networks, software integration, and mobile hardware.
* **Global Business Administration:** Covers international trade, finance, and management to train global corporate leaders.

### Korean Language Program
The **Korean Language House (KLH)** offers 10-week intensive language courses (four terms/year) with cultural activities.

### Scholarships for International Students
DKU provides merit-based aid:
1. **Admission Scholarship:** Offers a **30% to 60% tuition waiver** for the first semester based on TOPIK (Level 3+) or IELTS/TOEFL scores.
2. **Academic Excellence Scholarship:** From the second semester, maintaining a 3.0+ GPA (on a 4.5 scale) can secure **up to 100% tuition coverage**.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

DKU admissions prioritize academic preparation and language readiness.

### Application Requirements
* **Academics:** High school graduation certificate and transcripts (apostilled/consular-verified).
* **Language:** TOPIK Level 3+ (Korean track) or IELTS 5.5 / TOEFL iBT 80+ (English track).
* **Financials:** Bank balance certificate of at least $20,000 USD.

### Application Timeline
* **Spring Entry (March):** Apply September – November.
* **Fall Entry (September):** Apply March – May.

## 7. FAQ

### What is the difference between the Jukjeon and Cheonan campuses?
Jukjeon focuses on IT, engineering, software, and humanities, while Cheonan hosts life sciences, medicine, and languages. Students are assigned based on major.

### Do I need to speak fluent Korean?
No. English-taught tracks require only English proficiency (e.g., TOEFL/IELTS). However, basic Korean is helpful for daily life.

### What is the acceptance rate?
The international acceptance rate is about **40%**. Qualified applicants meeting minimum requirements stand a strong chance.

### Are dorms guaranteed for freshmen?
Yes, DKU prioritizes international freshmen for Woongbi Hall, provided they apply during the housing registration window.

### Can international students work part-time?
Yes. D-2 visa holders can work up to 20 hours per week during semesters with university and immigration approval. TOPIK Level 2+ is generally required.
