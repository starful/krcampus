## 1. School Overview

The Youngsan University (Y'sU) Korean Language Institute in Busan (142 Bansong-ro, Haeundae-gu) accommodates up to 400 international students annually. Operating directly on the Haeundae Campus, it blends high-quality language coursework with immersive cultural education. The program is ideal for students aiming to matriculate into Korean undergraduate programs or acquire professional-level language skills. Key benefits include targeted TOPIK preparation, direct university pathway scholarships, guaranteed on-campus dormitory facilities, and structured regional field trips designed to introduce students to the culture of Busan.

---

## 2. Programs & Schedule

The institute runs a quarterly academic system with four 10-week semesters annually. Classes meet for 20 hours per week (Monday to Friday, 4 hours per day) for a total of 200 hours per semester. Levels range from beginner (Level 1) to advanced (Level 6), with instruction split into morning and afternoon sessions depending on placement test results.

## 4. Admissions & D-4 Visa Steps

International applicants must follow a structured process to secure admission and obtain a D-4 General Student Visa.

1. **Apply & Submit Documents:** Submit the completed application form, passport copy, photos, high school graduation certificate (apostilled or consular-verified), and a bank statement showing at least $10,000 USD.
2. **Screening & Interview:** The admissions board conducts document verification and an online interview to evaluate academic intent.
3. **Tuition Payment:** Approved applicants receive a tuition invoice and must wire the required fees (usually 2–4 semesters) directly to the university's bank account.
4. **Receive Certificate of Admission (CoA):** Upon payment verification, the university issues the CoA and the school's business registration certificate.
5. **Visa Application:** The student submits the CoA, business registration, passport, visa application, and health records (including a tuberculosis test) to their local Korean Embassy or Consulate.
6. **Arrival:** Once the D-4 visa is granted, students coordinate dormitory check-in and travel to Busan.

## 5. TOPIK & University Pathway

The curriculum integrates target test preparation to help students master the TOPIK (Test of Proficiency in Korean) exam, which is a key requirement for long-term academic and professional success in South Korea.

### Undergraduate Pathway Scholarships
Students who complete at least two semesters of the language program and achieve TOPIK Level 3 (or Level 2 for physical education/arts majors) can matriculate directly into Youngsan University’s degree programs. Benefits include:
* **Application Fee Waiver:** Free processing for university admission.
* **Tuition Scholarships:** First-semester tuition discounts ranging from 30% to 50% or more, determined by the certified TOPIK level achieved.
* **Visa Transition:** Direct administrative support when transitioning from a D-4 (Language) to a D-2 (Degree) student status.

## 6. Dormitory & Living in the City

### On-Campus Dormitory
The university prioritizes housing for international students. Shared rooms (double/quad occupancy) include individual beds, study desks, closets, and climate control. Shared facilities include laundromats, gyms, study halls, and communal kitchens. The cost ranges from 800,000 to 1,000,000 KRW per 16-week period.

### Living in Haeundae, Busan
Located at 142 Bansong-ro, the Haeundae Campus is connected directly to Busan Metro Line 4 via Youngsan University Station. This allows fast, low-cost transit to major city centers, Centum City shopping complexes, and Haeundae Beach. 

### Busan Cultural Experience Tours
To aid language retention and integration, the institute hosts quarterly field trips. Activities include visits to historic Gyeongju sites, Gamcheon Culture Village, temple tours, and local culinary excursions. Travel and entry expenses are built directly into the tuition fee.

## 7. FAQ

### Is class attendance mandatory?
Yes. Students must maintain a minimum of 80% attendance. Falling below this threshold leads to course failure and blocks D-4 visa extensions at the immigration office.

### Can I work part-time while studying?
Yes, after completing six months (two semesters) of study. You must maintain at least 90% class attendance, achieve a basic TOPIK level, and obtain authorization from both the school and the Korean Immigration Office.

### Is the dormitory guaranteed?
Yes, on-campus dormitory placement is highly prioritized and guaranteed for all newly entering language students who apply during the standard registration window.

### What is the refund policy if my visa is denied?
If your D-4 visa application is rejected by the Korean Embassy, the university refunds the full tuition. The 50,000 KRW application fee remains non-refundable.

### How do I get to the campus from Gimhae Airport?
Take the Gimhae Light Rail to Sasang Station, transfer to Metro Line 2 to Dongnae, and then transfer to Metro Line 4 to exit at Youngsan University Station. A taxi takes approximately 50 minutes and costs about 40,000 KRW.
