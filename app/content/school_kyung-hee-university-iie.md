# Kyung Hee University Institute of International Education (IIE): A Guide

## 1. School Overview
The Institute of International Education (IIE) at Kyung Hee University is a premier Korean language center located at **26, Kyungheedae-ro, Dongdaemun-gu, Seoul, 02447, Republic of Korea**. With an annual capacity of over **2,000 students**, the institute is renowned for its academic excellence and its stunning, historic Gothic-style campus.

Key features of the institute include:
* **Prestigious and Historic Campus:** A beautiful learning environment with historic landmarks.
* **D-4 General Student Visa Sponsorship:** Offered for students enrolled in long-term programs.
* **Active Buddy Program:** Matches international students with domestic Kyung Hee students.
* **TOPIK Preparation and Dedicated Support:** Focused resources for language proficiency exams.
* **High-Quality Dormitory Accommodation Available:** Convenient on-campus housing options.
* **Various Korean Cultural Experiences:** Immersive activities including traditional arts and field trips.

## 2. Programs & Schedule
The IIE provides highly structured curricula targeting all language proficiencies (Levels 1 to 6). Regular programs require a minimum of 20 hours per week (4 hours daily, Monday to Friday) for 10 weeks, totaling 200 hours per term.

## 4. Admissions & D-4 Visa Steps
International applicants aiming to study for more than one term typically need to secure a D-4 General Student Visa. Follow these steps:

1. **Submit Application:** Complete the online form on the KHU IIE portal and upload your passport, graduation certificate (apostilled/consular-verified), and a bank statement showing at least $10,000 USD.
2. **Document Screening:** The admission office reviews the documents (takes 2-3 weeks).
3. **Tuition Payment:** Once accepted, transfer the designated tuition to the school's account.
4. **CoA Issuance:** Receive the official Certificate of Admission (CoA).
5. **Visa Application:** Bring the CoA, application form, and passport to your local Korean Embassy to receive your D-4 visa.

## 5. TOPIK & University Pathway
KHU IIE is a designated official TOPIK test venue. The institute offers targeted TOPIK preparation courses focusing on mock tests, writing techniques, and exam strategies.

Students who complete Level 3 or 4 of the Regular Program are eligible for pathways into undergraduate or graduate programs at Kyung Hee University. Many university departments accept IIE course completion certificates in lieu of external TOPIK scores, streamlining your entry into Korean higher education.

## 6. Dormitory & Living in the City
On-campus and near-campus dormitory facilities (such as Woo-Jung Won) offer premium rooms equipped with beds, desks, wardrobes, and high-speed Wi-Fi. Double rooms cost approximately 1,200,000 to 1,800,000 KRW per semester.

The campus is situated in Dongdaemun-gu, a vibrant area adjacent to Hoegi Station (Line 1). The student-centered neighborhood offers budget-friendly dining halls, cafes, and rapid public transit connections to downtown Seoul.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I work part-time on a D-4 student visa?
Yes. You can work part-time after six months of study, provided you have at least 90% class attendance and receive approval from the university and immigration office.

### Is the application fee refundable?
No. The 60,000 KRW application fee is non-refundable regardless of admission results or enrollment status.

### How are students assigned to their starting levels?
All incoming students must take a mandatory placement test (written and oral) before classes start to determine their level.

### Is health insurance required?
Yes. International students must carry health insurance. You are automatically enrolled in the National Health Insurance Service (NHIS) after obtaining your Alien Registration Card.

### What is the class attendance requirement?
Students must maintain at least an 80% attendance rate. Falling below this threshold will result in academic failure and inability to renew your visa.
