# Youngsan University (YSU) International Student Guide

Choosing the right study-abroad destination is a pivotal decision. For students seeking high-quality education, practical training, and an exciting lifestyle, **Youngsan University (YSU)** in Busan, South Korea, stands out. With its specialized Haeundae campus, industry-aligned curricula, and financial aid, YSU offers an accessible, career-oriented path for global applicants.

---

## 1. University Overview

Youngsan University (영산대학교) is a private institution dedicated to cultivating practical professionals. Operating dual campuses, its international hub is the **Haeundae Campus** in Busan (*142 Bansong-ro, Haeundae-gu*), placing students in South Korea's maritime and tourism capital.

With an **acceptance rate of ~80%**, YSU is highly accessible to qualified international applicants. It hosts over **1,250 international students** from diverse backgrounds, fostering a multicultural environment. YSU focuses heavily on employability, leveraging regional strengths in tourism, smart tech, and digital contents.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

While Korean is the primary language, YSU offers specialized pathways to support international students:

*   **Global Business & Tourism Tracks:** Select courses within the College of Global Business and College of Hotel & Tourism Management feature English-medium instruction or English-friendly study materials.
*   **Korean Language Program (KLP):** YSU’s Language Education Center runs intensive 10-week semesters guiding beginners to TOPIK Level 3 or 4, facilitating entry into undergraduate programs.
*   **Dual-Language Support:** International freshmen are paired with bilingual senior mentors to help navigate academics and life in Busan.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

YSU evaluates applicants holistically based on academic records, language skills, and motivation.

### Key Requirements:
1.  **Academic Records:** High school graduation certificate for Bachelor's applicants; a Bachelor's degree for Graduate applicants.
2.  **Language Proficiency:**
    *   *Korean Track:* TOPIK Level 3 (Level 2 for certain arts/physical majors).
    *   *English Track:* TOEFL iBT 71, IELTS 5.5, or CEFR B2.
3.  **Financial Solvency:** Bank balance of $16,000 to $18,000 USD (held for at least one month) for the D-2 visa.

### Application Steps:
*   **Step 1:** Submit the online application and academic transcripts.
*   **Step 2:** Pay the application processing fee.
*   **Step 3:** Complete an online admission interview (in Korean or English).
*   **Step 4:** Receive the Certificate of Admission (CoA), pay tuition, and apply for a D-2 visa.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

Studying at YSU's Haeundae Campus offers an excellent balance of education and lifestyle.

*   **Prime Location:** Located at *142 Bansong-ro*, the campus connects directly to Busan’s subway system. Beaches, traditional markets, and shopping districts are a short ride away.
*   **Dormitories:** International students receive priority housing. Rooms feature Wi-Fi, AC, and heating. Shared facilities include gyms, lounges, and laundries.
*   **Industry Ties:** Students benefit from regional partnerships, engaging in hospitality internships, design field trips, and collaboration projects with local tech firms.

## 7. FAQ

### What is the acceptance rate for international students at Youngsan University?
The acceptance rate is approximately 80%. YSU is accessible to international applicants who meet basic academic and visa requirements.

### Are there opportunities to work part-time while studying at YSU?
Yes. D-2 visa holders can work part-time (up to 20 hours per week during semesters, unlimited during vacations) after completing one semester, subject to university and immigration approval.

### Is on-campus housing guaranteed for international freshmen?
Yes. International freshmen are prioritized for dormitory placement to help them adjust smoothly to living in South Korea.

### Can I apply to YSU if I do not speak any Korean?
Yes. You can enroll in the Korean Language Program (KLP) before starting your major, or apply directly to English-taught tracks in Global Business.

### What are the main advantages of studying in Busan compared to Seoul?
Busan offers a significantly lower cost of living for housing and food while providing excellent transit, culture, and career opportunities in tourism and hospitality.
