## University Overview
Youngsan University (YSU) is a private university located in the southeastern region of South Korea. It operates two distinct campuses: the **Haeundae Campus in Busan** (focusing on Tourism, Hospitality, MICE, and Cultural Contents) and the **Yangsan Campus in South Gyeongsang Province** (focusing on Engineering, Health Sciences, and Public Administration). For international students who dream of studying in Korea's most famous beach city, the Haeundae Campus offers an unparalleled lifestyle combined with specialized, career-oriented education.

Historically, YSU has distinguished itself through specialized vocational education, practical industry integration, and a highly welcoming environment for global learners. With over 1,200 international students from various corners of Asia, Europe, and the Americas, YSU offers a robust support network, rich cultural experiences, and structured pathways to local employment.

## Global Strengths & Key Specializations

### 1. World-Class Hospitality and Tourism Education
Situated in Haeundae, Busan’s premier tourism hub, Youngsan University boasts one of the most prestigious **Hotel & Tourism Management** colleges in Korea. Students gain hands-on training in state-of-the-art mock hotel rooms, casinos, and culinary labs. The university maintains deep partnerships with luxury hotels (like the Hyatt, Westin Josun, and Paradise Hotel Busan) and major airline networks, offering extensive internship opportunities.

### 2. Rising Creative Hub: Webtoon, Animation, and Game Design
Busan is a thriving media city, hosting the Busan International Film Festival (BIFF). YSU has capitalized on this by building an elite **College of Creative Arts & Tech**. The **Webtoon Department** and **Game Design Department** are highly sought after, featuring industry-standard equipment and classes taught by actively publishing webtoon artists and game developers.

### 3. Industry-Tailored Global Business Programs
The Global Business faculty focuses on preparing international students for real-world global trade, logistics, and management. By blending theoretical studies with local industrial site visits in the Busan-Ulsan-Gyeongnam metropolitan area (the industrial heartland of Korea), students graduate with practical insights that make them highly employable.

## Practical Information for International Applicants

### Language Requirements & Track Selection
*   **Korean Track:** Most mainstream majors require a minimum of **TOPIK Level 3** (or completion of the university's Level 3 Korean Language program). For graduation, students are typically expected to reach TOPIK Level 4.
*   **English Track:** Several programs in Global Business and Hospitality offer courses taught in English. Requirements generally include an official score of IELTS 5.5, TOEFL iBT 71, or equivalent.

### Generous Scholarship System
Youngsan University is well-known for reducing the financial burden on international students through its proactive scholarship offerings:
*   **Admission Scholarship:** Based on your initial TOPIK score, you can receive a **30% to 50% tuition waiver** for your first semester. (e.g., TOPIK 3 typically yields 30% reduction, while TOPIK 5 or 6 can yield up to 50-60%).
*   **Academic Excellence Scholarship:** From the second semester onward, students can maintain a 30% to 50% tuition discount by keeping their GPA above 3.0/4.5.
*   **Korean Language Improvement Incentive:** Additional cash rewards or tuition grants are provided to students who upgrade their TOPIK levels during their enrollment.

## Campus Life and Student Support in Busan
Living in Haeundae, Busan, offers a perfect blend of modern city conveniences, scenic coastal views, and rich cultural heritage. 

*   **On-Campus Dormitories:** YSU provides secure, affordable, and fully furnished housing for international students. Facilities include high-speed internet, study rooms, gymnasiums, and communal kitchens.
*   **International Support Center (ISC):** The ISC provides comprehensive one-stop services, assisting with visa extensions, alien registration, health insurance, part-time job permits, and counseling.
*   **Cultural Exchange Programs:** Global students can participate in Korean cultural excursions, temple stays, K-pop dance classes, and tandem language-exchange programs with local Korean students.
