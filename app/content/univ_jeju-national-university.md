## University Overview

Jeju National University (JNU), founded in 1952, is the leading flagship public national university on South Korea's premier resort island, Jeju. JNU is highly regarded for offering a top-tier, research-oriented education at a fraction of the cost of private institutions in Seoul. Surrounded by pristine beaches, volcanic landscapes, and lush hiking trails, the university offers a serene, inspiring, and uniquely balanced environment for international students who wish to escape the hyper-congested mainland cities while earning a respected degree.

The university is highly committed to globalization, offering extensive support networks, Korean language programs, and specialized fields of study tailored to the local strengths of Jeju Island, including Marine Biology, Tourism Management, and Sustainable Energy.

## Academics & Unique Strengths

Due to its unique geographical positioning, Jeju National University hosts world-class programs in disciplines that are highly relevant to its ecosystem:

*   **Marine & Ocean Sciences:** Positioned in the middle of the East China Sea, JNU boasts state-of-the-art research vessels and labs specializing in marine biodiversity, aquaculture, and climate change.
*   **Tourism & Hospitality Management:** As Jeju Island is a global tourist hub, students in this department gain access to invaluable internship opportunities with luxury resorts, international casinos, and duty-free enterprises.
*   **Veterinary Medicine & Applied Life Sciences:** Highly competitive programs that specialize in equestrian sciences, animal welfare, and subtropical agriculture.
*   **Smart Grid & Green Technology:** Jeju operates as a testbed for South Korea's carbon-free island initiatives, making JNU's engineering programs excellent hubs for studying electric vehicles, wind power, and smart grids.

## Admissions & Requirements for International Students

International undergraduate admissions are held twice a year (Spring and Fall semesters). 

### Language Requirements
*   **Korean Track:** Most majors require a **TOPIK Level 3** or higher. Some competitive fields like Veterinary Medicine or Nursing require TOPIK Level 5 or higher.
*   **English Track:** Limited options are available for students presenting valid TOEFL, IELTS, or TEPS scores, although learning basic Korean is strongly encouraged to navigate daily life on the island.

## Cost of Living & Campus Facilities

Jeju Island offers a significantly lower cost of living compared to Seoul. 

### Dormitories
JNU prioritizes international students for on-campus housing. The modern dormitory complex is exceptionally affordable, costing approximately **₩200,000 to ₩350,000 per month**, which typically includes options for meal plans. Facilities include fitness centers, study lounges, laundry rooms, and high-speed internet.

### Daily Life and Transport
While Jeju is an island, its public bus network seamlessly connects the university campus to both downtown Jeju City and the southern coastal city of Seogwipo. Students enjoy a relaxed lifestyle, taking advantage of surfing, trekking, and cafe hopping during their weekends.
