# Pusan National University Korean Language Program: Ultimate Practical Guide

Choosing the right institute to study Korean is a critical step for your academic and career pathway. The **Pusan National University (PNU) Korean Language Education Center** stands out as a premier destination in southern South Korea, offering a high-quality curriculum backed by national university resources.

---

## 1. School Overview

Located in the coastal city of Busan, PNU is one of Korea’s top-tier national universities. The Language Education Center offers a highly structured environment to help international students transition from complete beginners to academic-level speakers.

*   **Address:** Language Education Center (Bldg. 201), Pusan National University, 2 Busandaehak-ro 63beon-gil, Geumjeong-gu, Busan, 46241, Republic of Korea
*   **Student Capacity:** 1,000 students per year
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **National University Prestige:** High academic standards and rich campus facilities.
    *   **Affordable Tuition:** Program fees are significantly lower than private universities in Seoul.
    *   **Dormitory Guarantee:** Priority housing is allocated for new language students.
    *   **TOPIK Preparation:** Tailored curricula directly target the official proficiency test.
    *   **Korean Buddy Program:** Connects international students with local PNU undergraduates.

---

## 2. Programs & Schedule

The program operates on a quarterly basis with four terms per year: Spring, Summer, Autumn, and Winter. The curriculum features six proficiency levels (Level 1 to Level 6) aligned with the official TOPIK framework. 

Each term runs for 10 weeks, totaling 200 hours of instruction (20 hours per week, Monday through Friday, 4 hours per day). Classes are split into morning (09:00 - 13:00) and afternoon (13:30 - 17:30) sessions based on placement results.

## 4. Admissions & D-4 Visa Steps

To study for more than 90 days, you must obtain a **D-4 (General Student) Visa** by registering for at least two terms (20 weeks).

### Step-by-Step Admission Process:

1.  **Online Application:** Register on the PNU website and upload scanned required documents.
2.  **Evaluation:** The admissions committee reviews academic and financial capacity.
3.  **Application Fee:** Pay the non-refundable 60,000 KRW fee.
4.  **Tuition Payment:** Upon receiving preliminary approval, wire the tuition (minimum 2 semesters: 2,800,000 KRW).
5.  **Admission Letter (CoA):** PNU sends the official Certificate of Admission (CoA).
6.  **Visa Application:** Submit the CoA, passport, academic documents, and a bank statement showing $10,000+ USD to your local Korean Embassy.

---

## 5. TOPIK & University Pathway

PNU focuses on academic progression, maintaining high TOPIK pass rates through exam prep classes in Levels 4 to 6.

*   **Admission Perks:** Completing Level 4 or higher waives the language requirement for PNU undergraduate applications.
*   **Tuition Scholarships:** PNU undergraduate programs offer partial tuition waivers to international students with high-level TOPIK certificates (Level 4 or above).

## 6. Dormitory & Living in the City

### On-Campus Dormitory Guarantee
PNU provides guaranteed housing for new language students during their first semester.
*   **Room Type:** Twin shared room with desk, chair, wardrobe, AC, Wi-Fi, and shared laundry facilities.
*   **Cost:** Approx. 1,000,000 to 1,300,000 KRW per semester (includes meal plan options).

### Living in Geumjeong-gu, Busan
Busan is South Korea’s second-largest city, combining urban convenience with beaches. The campus is in Geumjeong-gu, a vibrant university district.
*   **Transportation:** Located next to PNU Subway Station (Line 1), giving quick access to Seomyeon, Haeundae Beach, and Busan Station.
*   **Culture:** The area features budget-friendly cafes, restaurants, study spaces, and shops.
*   **Integration:** The **Korean Buddy Program** pairs you with a local PNU student to practice language and navigate daily life.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I work part-time while studying at PNU under a D-4 visa?
Yes. After studying for six months (two semesters) with an attendance rate of 90% or higher, you can apply for an S-3 part-time work permit to work up to 20 hours per week.

### What is the refund policy if my D-4 visa is rejected?
If officially denied, PNU will refund your tuition fees (minus the application fee) upon submission of the official visa refusal document.

### Are textbooks included in the 1,400,000 KRW tuition?
No. Textbooks must be purchased separately at the on-campus bookstore for approximately 50,000 KRW per level.

### Is the dormitory guaranteed for the entire duration of my studies?
No. It is guaranteed for the first semester only. For subsequent terms, assignment depends on your cumulative grades, attendance, and room availability.

### Do I need to know Korean before applying?
No. Level 1 starts with the Korean alphabet (Hangeul). All students take a placement test upon arrival to ensure they study at the appropriate level.
