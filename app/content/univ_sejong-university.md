## 1. University Overview

Established in 1940, Sejong University (세종대학교) is a prestigious private university in Seoul. Named after King Sejong the Great, the creator of Hangul, it is globally recognized for its rapid academic rise and strong reputation in specialized fields. Hosting over 3,200 international students from 50+ countries, Sejong maintains a balanced international acceptance rate of 35% to 45%. The campus integrates traditional Korean architecture with advanced research centers, offering a future-oriented learning environment.

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

Sejong University offers comprehensive English-track undergraduate and graduate programs taught by faculty holding degrees from elite global institutions.

### Popular English-Track Programs:
*   **BBA in Business Administration:** AACSB-accredited program covering global management, finance, and marketing.
*   **BS in Hospitality and Tourism Management:** Flagship program providing global networks and practical training.
*   **BS in Computer Science & Engineering / Software Convergence:** Focuses on AI, data science, and modern software development.

For bilingual or Korean-track students, the Sejong Center for International Education (SCIE) provides intensive Korean language courses alongside major academic studies to ensure a smooth transition.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

Admissions open twice a year for Spring (March) and Fall (September) semesters.

### Step-by-Step Application Process:
1.  **Online Application:** Submit applicant details via the official international portal.
2.  **Document Submission:** Mail physical, apostilled transcripts, graduation certificates, and family relations proof.
3.  **Financial Verification:** Provide a bank statement showing at least $16,000 USD to comply with D-2 visa rules.
4.  **Language Proficiency:** Submit official TOPIK, IELTS, or TOEFL scores.
5.  **Interview:** Some arts/physical education programs require an online portfolio review or virtual interview.

## 7. FAQ

### Q1: Can I work part-time while studying at Sejong University?
Yes. D-2 visa holders can work up to 20 hours per week during semesters (unlimited during breaks) after receiving school and immigration approval. Higher TOPIK scores unlock more hours and job types.

### Q2: What is the minimum TOPIK score required for Korean-taught majors?
Most Korean-track majors require a minimum of **TOPIK Level 3** for admission, though achieving Level 4 or higher is recommended before graduation.

### Q3: Are there on-campus housing options for international freshmen?
Yes. Sejong guarantees priority placement in **Saenal Hall** (the international dormitory) for incoming international freshmen during their first semester.

### Q4: How competitive is the acceptance rate for international applicants?
The estimated acceptance rate is **35% - 45%**. Admissions are holistic, evaluating academic performance, personal statements, and language proficiency.

### Q5: How prestigious is Sejong's Hospitality program compared to other universities?
It is ranked #1 in South Korea and **top 50 globally** (QS Rankings). The program partners with major global hotels, offering premier domestic and international internship opportunities.
