# Ultimate Study Abroad Guide: Seoul National University (SNU)

As an expert study-abroad consultant, I highly recommend Seoul National University (SNU) for ambitious students seeking world-class academics and cutting-edge research. Established in 1946, SNU is the prestigious "S" in the "SKY" university trio, representing the peak of Korean higher education.

---

## 1. University Overview

Located at **1 Gwanak-ro, Gwanak-gu, Seoul, 08826, Republic of Korea**, SNU is consistently ranked #1 in South Korea and top 40 globally. This elite status reflects its rigorous academic standards and remarkable global research output.

With a competitive **acceptance rate of 15%** for international applicants, SNU maintains an elite student body. It hosts over **3,800 international students** from 100+ countries. The university operates primarily on its expansive 4.3 square-kilometer Gwanak Campus. Nestled near Gwanak Mountain, the campus offers a unique mix of natural beauty and immediate access to metropolitan Seoul.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

While Korean is the primary language, SNU has expanded its global footprint with a diverse selection of English-taught programs and globally-recognized faculty.

* **Undergraduate Level:** Fully English-taught degrees are rare, but departments offer 10% to 30% of their courses in English. The College of Business Administration and College of Liberal Studies offer the highest density of English courses.
* **Graduate Level:** The **Graduate School of International Studies (GSIS)** offers fully English-taught master's and Ph.D. programs. The SNU Business School also offers an English-medium MBA.
* **Language Support:** SNU’s Language Education Institute (LEI) offers intensive Korean courses to help international scholars transition smoothly.

### Scholarship Opportunities
SNU offers excellent scholarships to attract top international talent:
1. **SNU Global Scholarship:** Covers tuition (full/partial), living expenses (1.2M KRW/month), and round-trip airfare.
2. **President Fellowship:** Aimed at faculty members from developing countries pursuing a Ph.D.
3. **GSFS Scholarship:** Provides tuition waivers and monthly stipends for outstanding graduate students from Asian countries.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

SNU divides international admission into **International Admissions I** (parents are non-Korean) and **International Admissions II** (applicant completed all education abroad).

### Key Requirements:
* **Academic Transcripts:** Exceptional GPA from previous institutions.
* **Language Proficiency:** TOPIK Level 3+ or TOEFL iBT 80+ / IELTS 6.0+ for English-tracked majors.
* **Standardized Tests:** Scores like SAT, ACT, AP, or IB are highly recommended for undergraduates.
* **References:** Two academic letters of recommendation.

The application window for the Fall semester opens in March, while the Spring semester window opens in July of the preceding year.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

The 4.3 square-kilometer Gwanak Campus is a vibrant academic city. It features state-of-the-art libraries, high-tech research labs, gymnasiums, and the SNU Museum of Art.

### Living in Gwanak-gu
The campus sits at the foot of Gwanak Mountain, providing incredible scenery. Below the campus lies Gwanak-gu, packed with student-friendly cafes, international eateries, and trendy study spots like "Sharosugil."

With the SNU Subway Station (Line 2) linked via frequent campus shuttles, students can reach Gangnam, Hongdae, or downtown Seoul in under 30 minutes, balancing focused study with dynamic urban life.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I study at SNU if I don't speak fluent Korean?
Yes. You can apply to fully English-taught graduate programs (like GSIS or GS-MBA). However, studying basic Korean at the SNU Language Education Institute prior to your semester is highly recommended.

### What is the acceptance rate for international applicants?
The international acceptance rate is around 15%. However, highly competitive fields like Computer Science and Business Administration have much lower acceptance rates, often dropping below 5%.

### How do I apply for the SNU Global Scholarship?
You can apply during your regular SNU online admission application. There is a specific scholarship section where you must upload required supporting financial and academic documents.

### Are international students allowed to work part-time in South Korea?
Yes. Students on a D-2 visa can work part-time for up to 20 hours per week during semesters (unlimited during breaks) with university and immigration approval. High TOPIK scores make finding jobs much easier.

### What is the cost of living on or near the Gwanak Campus?
Students should budget roughly 1,200,000 to 1,500,000 KRW monthly ($900 - $1,150 USD) for housing, food, and transport. On-campus dormitories (Gwanak-sa) are cheaper but highly competitive.
