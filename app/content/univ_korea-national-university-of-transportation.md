## University Overview

Korea National University of Transportation (KNUT) is South Korea's premier and only national university specializing in transportation, logistics, and railway systems engineering. Established through the merger of several historic technical colleges, KNUT has carved out a unique niche in the Korean higher education landscape. While its specialized railway campus is located in Uiwang, the primary, sprawling campus is nestled in the scenic city of Chungju, Chungcheongbuk-do.

As a national university, KNUT is funded directly by the Ministry of Education, allowing it to offer top-tier academic resources, cutting-edge laboratory facilities, and exceptionally low tuition fees compared to private universities in the Seoul Metropolitan Area.

## Why Choose Korea National University of Transportation?

* **Unrivaled Specialization**: If your career aspirations lie in high-speed rail, smart mobility, logistics, or transportation planning, KNUT is the undisputed leader in Korea. It maintains deep ties with corporate giants like Hyundai Rotem, KORAIL, and the Korea Railroad Research Institute (KRRI).
* **Highly Affordable Education**: Tuition rates at KNUT are roughly half those of private universities. Coupled with low living costs in Chungcheongbuk-do, it represents one of the most cost-effective routes to earning a respected Korean degree.
* **Modern Infrastructure and Global Research Labs**: Students have access to state-of-the-art simulation labs, wind tunnels, and autonomous vehicle testing environments.

## Campus Locations & Student Life

KNUT operates on a unique three-campus system, each dedicated to specific fields of study:

1. **Chungju Campus (Main)**: Focuses on Engineering, Humanities, Social Sciences, and Convergence Technologies. This is the heart of the university and home to most international students.
2. **Jeungpyeong Campus**: Dedicated to Health Sciences, Nursing, and Life Sciences.
3. **Uiwang Campus**: Located near Seoul, this is the historic base for Railway Engineering and Logistics.

International students enjoy a vibrant campus environment. The International Affairs Office organizes regular cultural excursions, Korean language exchange programs, and customized career counseling sessions to help students adapt to Korean society and find post-graduation employment.

## Scholarship Opportunities

KNUT offers generous financial aid packages to international applicants. New students can receive tuition waivers of up to 50% to 100% based on their TOPIK (Test of Proficiency in Korean) scores or English proficiency certificates (such as IELTS or TOEFL). Continuing students maintain these scholarships by maintaining a competitive GPA.
