# Ultimate Study Guide: Daegu University (대구대학교) for International Students

Daegu University (DU), established in 1956, is a premier private university located in Gyeongsan, Gyeongsangbuk-do, South Korea. Known for its dedication to social welfare, specialized education, and academic innovation, the university provides an incredibly welcoming environment for global talent seeking high-quality, affordable higher education.

---

## 1. University Overview

With a student-first philosophy, Daegu University boasts an average acceptance rate of 65% and is currently home to over 1,350 international students. The university is widely celebrated for its expansive and highly picturesque suburban campus in Gyeongsan. This green environment provides a tranquil, focused study atmosphere away from metropolitan congestion, while remaining fully connected to the cultural resources of Daegu metropolitan city. 

DU stands as a recognized national leader in Special Education and Rehabilitation Science, consistently ranking as the top training ground for healthcare, welfare, and educational specialists in South Korea. Combining historic values with future-oriented industries, DU has evolved into a comprehensive university preparing students for diverse global careers.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

Although Korean is the primary language of instruction, DU offers an expanding portfolio of English-taught courses, particularly within the College of Business Administration and the College of Engineering. 

For students who need to acquire language proficiency first, DU’s Korean Language Education Center (KLEC) offers outstanding intensive courses. The KLEC program guides students from absolute beginners to TOPIK Level 3 or higher, enabling smooth transitions into standard undergraduate majors. DU also coordinates dedicated global mentoring programs, intercultural exchanges, and English-track academic research initiatives to support international students from day one.

---

## 3. Faculties & Academic Strengths

DU offers diverse, career-oriented undergraduate programs across ten prestigious colleges:

*   **College of Education (Special Education):** The absolute national pioneer in training teachers for students with special needs, holding an unrivaled reputation in South Korea.
*   **College of Rehabilitation Science:** Renowned for clinical training in speech-language therapy, occupational therapy, and physical rehabilitation.
*   **College of Engineering & College of Information and Communication Engineering:** Focused on next-generation industries, providing cutting-edge tracks in smart IT, software, and advanced mechanical engineering.
*   **College of Business Administration & College of Social Sciences:** Focused on global commerce, marketing, public policy, and administrative services.
*   **College of Humanities:** Offering rich programs in languages, global communication, and cultural studies.
*   **College of Natural Sciences & College of Life and Environmental Sciences:** Promoting scientific research in biotechnology, agriculture, and environmental protection.
*   **College of Art and Design:** Cultivating creative designers, visual artists, and digital content creators.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

Applying to Daegu University involves a straightforward process managed by the Office of International Affairs.

*   **Academic Requirements:** Applicants must hold a high school graduation certificate (or equivalent) for undergraduate admissions.
*   **Language Requirements:** TOPIK Level 3 is standard for Korean-taught majors. For English tracks, a minimum of IELTS 5.5 or TOEFL iBT 80 is required.
*   **Timeline:** March Intake (applications close in November); September Intake (applications close in May).
*   **Required Documents:** Application form, apostilled graduation/transcript certificates, bank balance certificate (minimum USD 16,000), copy of passport, and family relationship certificate.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

Located at **201 Daegudae-ro, Jillyang-eup, Gyeongsan-si, Gyeongsangbuk-do**, DU's campus is famous for its natural beauty. It features scenic cherry-blossom pathways and the serene Muncheonji Lake, earning it a reputation as one of the most picturesque campuses in Korea.

International students live primarily in the "Biho Yeoga" dormitories, which provide fitness centers, study lounges, global kitchens, and 24-hour security. Gyeongsan acts as a student-friendly university town. Downtown Daegu is easily accessible via direct city buses and nearby subway links, giving students the perfect balance of a peaceful study environment and vibrant urban life.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I apply if I do not speak any Korean?
Yes. You can enroll in DU’s Korean Language Education Center (KLEC) to build your language skills. Once you achieve TOPIK Level 3, you can easily matriculate into your desired undergraduate program.

### Is on-campus housing guaranteed for international students?
Yes, Daegu University guarantees dormitory placement for all incoming international freshmen during their first year, provided they apply during the housing application window.

### Can international students work part-time?
Yes. With a D-2 student visa, you can work part-time up to 20 hours per week during semesters (and unlimited hours during vacations) after obtaining permission from the immigration office and your academic advisor.

### Why is DU's Special Education program highly rated?
DU was founded with a mission of social welfare. It was the first Korean university to establish dedicated special education and rehabilitation departments, offering unmatched professional facilities and clinical training.

### How do I travel from campus to downtown Daegu?
The campus is connected to central Daegu via frequent city buses that link directly to local subway stations (Ansim and Yeungnam University stations). The commute takes about 45 to 50 minutes.
