## School Overview
Chungnam National University (CNU), founded in 1952, is one of South Korea's premier flagship national universities. Located in Daejeon, the scientific and technological heart of South Korea, CNU provides a world-class academic environment. 

The **Chungnam National University Korean Language Center** (International Language Education Center) offers highly structured and immersive Korean language programs designed for international students, diaspora Koreans, and global professionals. Combining exceptional academic quality with the highly affordable tuition rates typical of national universities, it is one of the most cost-effective and reputable centers outside of the Seoul metropolitan area.

## Program Curriculum

### Regular Program (10-Week Semesters)
The center runs a standard academic calendar divided into four terms: Spring, Summer, Fall, and Winter. Each term comprises 10 weeks of study, totaling 200 classroom hours.

* **Class Schedule:** Monday through Friday, 4 hours per day (usually 9:00 AM - 1:00 PM or 1:30 PM - 5:30 PM depending on level placement).
* **Level System:** Six levels (Level 1 to Level 6) covering beginner to advanced proficiencies. Classes emphasize the four core skills: speaking, listening, reading, and writing.
* **Small Class Sizes:** To maintain interaction quality, classes are capped at approximately 15 students.
* **TOPIK Support:** Special targeted prep sessions are offered to students aiming for university or graduate school admission in South Korea.

## Tuition and Living Costs
As a national university program, CNU offers significantly lower tuition compared to private institutions in Seoul:
* **Application Fee:** 60,000 KRW (non-refundable)
* **Tuition Fee:** 1,300,000 KRW per semester (subject to minor annual changes)
* **Living Costs:** Daejeon boasts a significantly lower cost of living compared to Seoul, making housing, food, and transport highly affordable for international students.

## Accommodation and Campus Life

### On-Campus Dormitories
International language students can apply to stay in the university's comfortable on-campus dormitories. 
* **Facilities:** Rooms are equipped with beds, desks, wardrobes, internet connections, and private or shared bathrooms. Cafeterias, convenience stores, and laundry facilities are located inside the residential complex.
* **Dormitory Fees:** Roughly 800,000 to 1,200,000 KRW per semester (varying by meal plan options and room types).

### Campus Facilities
Language students receive a student ID card, granting them access to all major campus facilities, including the massive central library, computing centers, athletic fields, gymnasium, and student healthcare clinics.

## Cultural Experiences & Support
CNU ensures that studying Korean extends far beyond the classroom:
* **Cultural Excursions:** Every semester, the center organizes cultural excursions, hands-on activities, and historic field trips. Students can participate in making traditional Korean crafts, wearing Hanbok, learning K-pop dance, and visiting historical sites.
* **Global Buddy Program:** International students are paired with CNU undergraduate students to practice conversational Korean, navigate campus life, and easily integrate into local Korean student culture.

## Location & Accessibility
CNU is located in Yuseong-gu, Daejeon. The campus is exceptionally spacious, flat, and filled with beautiful cherry blossoms in the spring. 
* **Public Transit:** The campus is served by Yuseong Spa Station (Daejeon Subway Line 1) and numerous local bus lines.
* **Travel to Seoul:** By KTX (high-speed train) from Daejeon Station or Seodaejeon Station, students can reach downtown Seoul in just under an hour.
