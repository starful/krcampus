## 1. School Overview
Established as a premier global hub for language learning, the Hankuk University of Foreign Studies (HUFS) Korean Language Education Center (KLEC) is located at **107, Imun-ro, Dongdaemun-gu, Seoul, Republic of Korea**. With a capacity of **1,200 students**, the center offers a highly structured environment for non-native speakers. HUFS is globally recognized for its advanced foreign language teaching methodologies. This foundation is integrated into its Korean curriculum, fostering a truly **Global Student Body** with students from over 50 countries, preventing any single nationality from dominating classrooms.

## 2. Programs & Schedule
HUFS KLEC provides courses from Level 1 (beginner) to Level 6 (advanced academic). Classes emphasize practical communication, grammar, and pronunciation.

*   **Regular Program (정규과정):** A 10-week (200 hours total) term offered quarterly. Classes run 5 days a week (Monday to Friday) for 4 hours daily. Designed for students seeking academic or professional fluency.
*   **Short-term Program (3-Week Intensive):** A highly focused, 3-week course running in August. It concentrates on speaking skills over a brief period.
*   **Korean Language and Culture Program:** A hybrid 3-week course in January and July combining intensive morning language studies with afternoon cultural excursions like traditional crafts and city tours.

## 4. Admissions & D-4 Visa Steps
International applicants studying for more than 90 days must secure a D-4 (General Student) visa. 

1. **Application:** Submit the online application, passport copy, graduation/enrollment certificate (apostilled or consular-verified), and a bank statement showing a balance of USD 10,000.
2. **Screening & Invoice:** The admissions office reviews documents (takes 2-3 weeks) and issues a tuition invoice.
3. **Payment:** Transfer the total invoiced amount via international wire transfer to the designated HUFS bank account.
4. **Admission Letter:** Upon payment confirmation, HUFS issues the Certificate of Admission (COA).
5. **Visa Application:** Present the COA, passport, and financial documents to the nearest Korean Embassy or Consulate to obtain your D-4 visa.

## 5. TOPIK & University Pathway
HUFS KLEC provides **Excellent University Admission Support** for students looking to progress to degree programs in Korea. 
*   **TOPIK Preparation:** Special TOPIK (Test of Proficiency in Korean) preparation electives and mock exams are offered during the regular terms to help students achieve Level 3 or 4 quickly.
*   **Admission Pathways:** KLEC graduates who complete Level 3 or higher receive official recommendation letters when applying to HUFS undergraduate programs. Achieving KLEC Level 4 or higher can satisfy the language proficiency requirement for university admission, bypassing the official TOPIK test in selected HUFS departments.

## 6. Dormitory & Living in the City
*   **Dormitory Access:** Registered students can apply for the **Globee Dorm** (on-campus) or international student houses. Rooms are fully furnished with study desks, beds, heating, air conditioning, and high-speed Wi-Fi. Spaces are allocated on a first-come, first-served basis.
*   **Living in Imun-dong:** Located in Dongdaemun-gu, the campus area offers a safe, student-friendly environment. Rent and dining are significantly more affordable than in central Seoul or southern Gangnam.
*   **Transportation:** The campus is a 5-minute walk from Hankuk University of Foreign Studies Station (Seoul Subway Line 1), allowing quick access to major cultural districts like Dongdaemun, Myeongdong, and Jongno.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I participate in language exchange activities with Korean students?
Yes. HUFS offers a **Language Exchange Partner Program** that pairs international students with HUFS domestic undergraduates. This is highly effective for practicing conversational Korean and making local friends.

### Can I apply for a D-4 student visa with the 3-week program?
No. The D-4 visa requires enrolling in a course that lasts at least one regular semester (10 weeks) and is typically granted only to those registering for two or more terms. Short-term students must enter on a C-3-9 tourist visa or K-ETA.

### Is there a placement test for new students?
Yes, a mandatory level placement test (written and oral interview) is conducted before classes begin. This ensures you are placed in a class that matches your actual Korean proficiency.

### Are textbooks included in the tuition?
No. Textbooks must be purchased separately. You can buy them at the HUFS bookstore on campus at the start of the semester for approximately 50,000 KRW.

### What is the average class size at HUFS KLEC?
To ensure quality interaction and personalized speaking feedback, classes are small, typically averaging 12 to 15 students per classroom.
