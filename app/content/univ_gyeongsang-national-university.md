# Gyeongsang National University (GNU) International Student Guide

Choosing the right university is a pivotal decision. Located in historic Jinju, Gyeongsang National University (GNU) is a premier South Korean destination offering world-class academics, highly affordable tuition, and a welcoming community for global scholars.

---

## 1. University Overview

Gyeongsang National University (GNU) is one of South Korea's **10 Flagship Korean National Universities (KNU)**. These elite institutions are government-funded to provide accessible, high-quality education while driving regional innovation and global research.

Following a landmark 2021 merger with Gyeongnam National University of Science and Technology (GNTECH), GNU consolidated its resources into a unified academic powerhouse. Today, GNU hosts around 550 international students from over 40 countries, maintaining an international **acceptance rate of 65%**. The university operates multiple campuses, with its primary headquarters in Jinju, Gyeongnam.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

While Korean is the primary language, GNU has expanded its global options to accommodate international students.

### Undergraduate English Options
Most undergraduate courses require TOPIK Level 3 or higher. However, selected core and elective courses within the **College of Engineering** (notably Aerospace Engineering) and the **College of Agriculture and Life Sciences** are taught in English.

### Graduate Programs (Master’s & Ph.D.)
Over 70% of graduate-level STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics) courses are conducted entirely in English, making GNU an excellent hub for global research.

### Korean Language Program
The GNU Language Education Center offers intensive Korean programs running four semesters a year (Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter) to help students reach academic TOPIK requirements.

*   **College of Law**
*   **College of Education**
*   **College of Veterinary Medicine**
*   **College of Medicine**
*   **College of Pharmacy**
*   **College of Nursing**
*   **College of Marine Science** (located in coastal Tongyeong)

### Specialized Academic Strengths

*   **Aerospace Engineering:** A national leader in aerospace technology, partnering with Korea Aerospace Industries (KAI) to offer direct internship and employment pathways.
*   **Agriculture & Life Sciences:** Focusing on bio-system technology, smart farming, and sustainable food resources, this faculty ranks among the top in Korea.
*   **Life Sciences & Veterinary Medicine:** Highly regarded programs featuring state-of-the-art animal hospitals and advanced molecular biology facilities.

## 4. Tuition, Fees & Scholarships

As a subsidized national university, GNU offers premium education at a fraction of private university costs. Notably, GNU has **eliminated its admission fee (0 KRW)**. The average annual tuition across all majors is approximately **4,100,000 KRW**.

### Scholarship Opportunities
*   **GNU Global Scholarship:** Offers 30% to 100% tuition waivers for incoming students based on academic records and TOPIK scores.
*   **Global Korea Scholarship (GKS):** Government-funded full ride covering tuition, monthly stipend, airfare, and medical insurance.
*   **Academic Excellence Award:** Awarded to enrolled students maintaining a GPA of 3.5/4.5 or higher.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

Applying to GNU involves a holistic evaluation of academic credentials, language proficiency, and a statement of purpose.

### Application Timeline

| Intake Semester | Application Period | Document Submission | Result Announcement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Spring Semester** (Starts March) | Mid-October to November | Mid-December | Late January |
| **Fall Semester** (Starts September) | Mid-April to May | Mid-June | Late July |

### Admission Requirements
1.  **Academic Credentials:** Graduation certificate and official transcripts (Apostille or Consular confirmation required).
2.  **Language Proficiency:** TOPIK Level 3+ for Korean tracks; TOEFL iBT 80, IELTS 5.5, or CEFR B2 for English tracks.
3.  **Financial Capacity:** A bank balance certificate showing a minimum of $18,000 USD held for at least one month.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

### The Historic City of Jinju
Located in Gyeongsangnam-do, Jinju is famous for its Namgang Yudeung (Lantern) Festival and Jinjuseong Fortress. It offers authentic Korean culture without the high costs of Seoul.

### Accommodation
GNU provides comfortable on-campus dorms featuring shared or single rooms, Wi-Fi, laundry facilities, and gym access. Housing costs approximately 1,000,000 to 1,500,000 KRW per semester, which often includes flexible meal plan options.

### Support & Integration
The Office of International & External Cooperation runs buddy programs, student clubs, and cultural excursions to help global students adjust smoothly.

## 7. FAQ

### What is the acceptance rate for international students at GNU?
The international acceptance rate is around **65%**. Applicants meeting basic language and academic documentation requirements have a very strong chance of admission.

### Are there opportunities to work part-time while studying?
Yes. D-2 visa holders can work up to 20 hours per week during semesters (and unlimited hours during vacations) with university and immigration approval. TOPIK requirements apply.

### Can I apply if I do not speak any Korean?
Yes. You can apply directly to English-taught graduate programs. For undergraduate programs, you can first enroll in the intensive Korean Language Program at GNU's Language Education Center to reach Level 3.

### How does the 2021 merger benefit international students?
The merger with GNTECH integrated research facilities, expanded majors, upgraded campus infrastructure, and boosted regional industry partnerships, providing superior career opportunities.

### How far is Jinju from Seoul and Busan?
Jinju is highly accessible. Seoul is about 3.5 hours away via KTX high-speed train or express bus, while Busan is just 1.5 hours away by intercity bus, making weekend trips convenient.
