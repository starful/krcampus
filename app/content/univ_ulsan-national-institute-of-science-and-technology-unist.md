## University Overview
Founded in 2007, the **Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology (UNIST)** has rapidly ascended to become one of the world's leading research-oriented universities. Alongside KAIST, GIST, and DGIST, UNIST is one of South Korea's four prestigious public institutes of science and technology. 

UNIST is renowned globally for its cutting-edge research in secondary batteries (energy storage), advanced materials, and chemical engineering. Its primary mission is to foster global leaders in science and technology while bridging the gap between academic innovation and industrial application.

## Why International Students Choose UNIST

### 1. 100% English-Only Education
Unlike many Korean universities where only select courses are taught in English, UNIST stands out by conducting **100% of its lectures, examinations, and coursework in English** for both undergraduate and graduate levels. This policy makes the academic transition seamless for international students and nurtures a truly global academic environment.

### 2. Full Scholarship Opportunities
UNIST ensures that financial barriers do not block top-tier international talent. 
- **Undergraduate Support:** The UNIST Scholarship covers the full tuition fee for students who maintain a standard GPA. In addition, students receive a monthly living stipend.
- **Graduate Support:** Almost all admitted international graduate students receive full tuition waivers along with handsome monthly stipends (from Research Assistantships/Teaching Assistantships) that easily cover housing, food, and personal expenses.

### 3. World-Class Research & Faculty
Despite its relatively short history, UNIST ranks incredibly high in research citations. According to major university rankings (such as the Times Higher Education and QS World University Rankings), UNIST's research impact routinely ranks top in South Korea. The campus houses state-of-the-art national research facilities, including the Center for Multidimensional Carbon Materials (part of the prestigious Institute for Basic Science - IBS).

### 4. Direct Industry Connections (The Ulsan Advantage)
Located in Ulsan, the industrial powerhouse of South Korea, UNIST collaborates closely with global giants like Hyundai Motor Group, Hyundai Heavy Industries, and SK Energy. This geographic and institutional advantage offers student-researchers direct pathways to internships, joint industrial projects, and post-graduation employment.

## Academic Programs and Structure
UNIST operates under a unique interdisciplinary system where departmental boundaries are minimized. Undergraduate students enter as 'undeclared' majors in either the School of Engineering or the School of Natural Sciences, allowing them to explore various subjects before selecting their specialized fields.

Key areas of academic excellence include:
- **Battery & Energy Engineering:** Consistently ranked among the top 3 globally alongside MIT and Stanford.
- **Carbon Materials & Chemistry:** Backed by world-class labs and heavily funded research centers.
- **Information Technology & AI:** Rapidly expanding computer science departments focusing on AI, big data, and smart manufacturing systems.

## Campus Life & Accommodation
UNIST's campus is modern, eco-friendly, and beautifully designed around Gamak Lake. Because the campus is located somewhat outside Ulsan's main urban center in a serene valley, campus life is highly community-focused.

- **Dormitories:** All international students are guaranteed housing in on-campus dormitories. The rooms are modern, equipped with heating and air conditioning, and affordable.
- **Global Clinic & Services:** The Global Center offers dedicated support for visa applications, health insurance, and cultural exchange activities to help international students integrate into South Korean society.
- **Facilities:** Includes a massive library, advanced fitness centers, cafeteria networks offering international/halal-friendly food options, and student-run clubs ranging from K-pop dance to robotic engineering.

## How to Apply
1. **Undergraduate Track:** Applications are submitted online during the spring/fall cycles. Requirements include high school transcripts, standardized test scores (optional but recommended), letters of recommendation, and proof of English proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS, though waived for native speakers and students from English-medium high schools).
2. **Graduate Track:** Focused strictly on research alignment. Applicants must choose and contact a prospective advising professor prior to or during the application cycle. Success highly correlates with academic output, GRE scores (optional), and compatibility with the lab's research goals.
