# Ultimate Study-Abroad Guide: Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST)

Choosing where to pursue higher education is a pivotal decision. For STEM-focused students, the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST) is one of the world's most prestigious options. This guide details everything international students need to know about admissions, finances, and student life at KAIST.

---

## 1. University Overview

Established in 1971 by the South Korean government with USAID support, KAIST was the nation's first research-oriented science and engineering institution. It has driven South Korea’s rapid economic and technological growth, earning its reputation as the "MIT of Asia."

*   **Location:** 291 Daehak-ro, Yuseong-gu, Daejeon, South Korea.
*   **Global Standing:** Consistently ranked in the top 40 globally (QS World Rankings) and top 20 for Engineering.
*   **Student Body:** Hosts over 1,100 international students from 90+ countries.
*   **Selectivity:** Highly competitive, with an estimated international acceptance rate of **15-20%**.

The main campus is located in **Daedeok Innopolis**, Daejeon’s premier R&D hub housing over 300 research institutions, offering students direct access to industry partnerships, internships, and research networks.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

Language is no barrier at KAIST: **virtually all undergraduate and graduate classes are conducted entirely in English.** Lectures, assignments, textbooks, and exams are in English. The campus is highly bilingual, and major administrative offices, counseling, and student portals provide comprehensive English services. While basic Korean is helpful for daily life off-campus, it is not an academic requirement.

To support international students, KAIST offers:
*   **Free Korean Language Courses:** Beginner to advanced levels.
*   **Global Initiative Programs:** Exchange opportunities with 100+ partner universities worldwide.
*   **Dual Degree Programs:** Partnerships with elite universities in the US, Europe, and Asia.

## 4. Tuition, Fees & Scholarships

KAIST offers generous financial aid to ensure that cost is not a barrier for international talent.

### KAIST International Student Scholarship
Most admitted international undergraduates receive the **KAIST International Student Scholarship**, which includes:
1.  **Full Tuition Waiver:** Covers all tuition for 8 semesters.
2.  **Monthly Stipend:** 350,000 KRW for living expenses.
3.  **National Health Insurance:** Subsidized or fully covered.

*Note: Scholarship renewal requires maintaining a cumulative GPA of 2.7/4.3.*

## 5. Admissions for International Students

KAIST uses a holistic admission process, evaluating academic achievements, potential, and passion for STEM.

### Application Timeline
*   **Early Track:** Opens around September (recommended for timely visa processing).
*   **Regular Track:** Opens around November/December.
*   **Late Track:** Opens around late February/March.

### Key Application Requirements
1.  **Online Application:** Submitted via the KAIST admissions portal.
2.  **Recommendation Letter:** Typically from a STEM teacher.
3.  **Transcripts:** Strong grades in advanced high school math and science.
4.  **Standardized Test Scores (Optional):** SAT, ACT, IB, AP, or national exam scores.
5.  **English Proficiency:** TOEFL iBT (min 83), IELTS (min 6.5), or equivalent (waived if high school was English-medium).
6.  **Financial Resources Statement:** Required even if applying for the scholarship.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

KAIST combines intensive academics with a supportive, vibrant campus community.

### Campus Facilities & Living
*   **Dormitories:** Guaranteed, highly affordable double-occupancy rooms on campus for international undergraduates.
*   **Sports:** Free access to fitness centers, pools, tennis courts, and climbing walls.
*   **Dining:** Subsidized on-campus cafeterias serving Halal, vegetarian, Korean, and Western cuisines.
*   **Support:** The **International Scholar and Student Services (ISSS)** provides airport pickups, visa guidance, and cultural activities.

### The Host City: Daejeon
Daejeon is South Korea's fifth-largest city, known for its high quality of life and scientific resources.
*   **Accessibility:** Located centrally, just 50 minutes from Seoul via high-speed KTX train.
*   **Affordability:** Living costs are significantly lower than in Seoul, making the monthly stipend stretch further.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I apply to KAIST if my high school did not offer AP or IB courses?
Yes. KAIST evaluates applications holistically. Strong performance in your school's national curriculum (e.g., A-Levels, CBSE, or local diplomas) is highly valued.

### What is the minimum GPA needed to keep the KAIST Scholarship?
You must maintain a cumulative GPA of **2.7 out of 4.3** (roughly a B- average) to retain the full tuition waiver and stipend.

### Are international students allowed to work part-time in Korea?
Yes. On a D-2 student visa, you can work part-time (typically up to 20 hours per week during semesters, and unlimited during breaks) with approval from the university and immigration office.

### Is there an application fee for KAIST?
Yes, an 80,000 KRW (approx. 60 USD) non-refundable online application fee is required. The matriculation/admission fee upon entry is 0 KRW.

### What are the career prospects for international KAIST graduates?
Graduates are highly sought after. Many enter elite global PhD programs, while others join leading tech firms like Samsung, Google, Hyundai, and Naver, or launch startups via KAIST's incubators.
