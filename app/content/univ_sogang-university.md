## 1. University Overview

Sogang University, founded in 1960 by the Society of Jesus (Jesuits), is one of South Korea's premier private research institutions. Located in Seoul, Sogang is globally recognized for its rigorous Jesuit academic tradition, which emphasizes holistic education, critical thinking, and social responsibility. With an international student body of approximately 3,200 students, the university serves as a diverse, vibrant hub for global education.

Sogang is distinguished by its unique "No-Barrier Multi-Major System (Unique in Korea)," a policy allowing students to freely combine up to three majors without grade-point requirements or department quotas. This flexibility, coupled with an elite reputation in business, economics, and humanities, places Sogang graduates at the forefront of the Korean corporate landscape.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

Sogang provides excellent pathways for students not yet fluent in Korean. The **School of Global Studies (SGS)** is the flagship undergraduate destination, offering fully English-taught degrees in Global Korean Studies and International Relations. At the graduate level, the Graduate School of International Studies (GSIS) and the Sogang Business School (SBS) offer extensive English-medium curricula.

For language learners, the highly acclaimed speaking-focused **KLEC (Korean Language Education Center)** program is globally renowned. KLEC’s communication-heavy teaching methodology ensures that international students rapidly develop conversational fluency, easing their transition into local degree programs and career opportunities in South Korea.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

Admissions at Sogang are competitive but accessible, with an estimated international acceptance rate of **25-30% (Estimated for International Applicants)**.

### Standard Admission Criteria:
1. **Academic Records:** Proof of high school graduation (equivalent to the Korean 12-year system).
2. **Language Requirements:**
   - **Korean Track:** TOPIK Level 3 or higher, or completion of Sogang KLEC Level 3 or higher.
   - **English Track (SGS):** TOEFL iBT 80, IELTS 5.5, or equivalent.
3. **Documents:** Personal statement, study plan, academic recommendations, and proof of financial capability (approx. USD 20,000).

Applications for the Spring semester close in October, while Fall applications close in April.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

Sogang's campus is at 35 Baekbeom-ro, Mapo-gu, Seoul, placing students inside the vibrant **Sinchon Student District**. This neighborhood is Seoul's collegiate heartland, shared with neighboring Yonsei and Ewha universities, offering budget-friendly eateries, cafes, and cultural venues.

On campus, international students reside in Gonzaga Hall, a modern residential facility. The compact, walkable campus allows students to cross from classrooms to the library in under ten minutes. Organizations like the Sogang Global Network (SGN) facilitate cultural integration through buddy systems, cultural nights, and excursions.

## 7. FAQ

### Can I apply to Sogang if I do not speak any Korean?
Yes. You can apply to the School of Global Studies (SGS), which offers completely English-taught undergraduate degree tracks. However, taking conversational courses at KLEC is recommended for daily life.

### How does the No-Barrier Multi-Major System work?
Sogang allows students to major in up to three different fields without any minimum GPA barriers, major-specific entry interviews, or department-enforced caps. This is a unique policy in Korea.

### Is on-campus dormitory housing guaranteed?
While not strictly guaranteed due to space limits, Gonzaga Hall rooms are prioritized for newly admitted international freshman applicants who submit their housing request on time.

### Are international students allowed to work part-time?
Yes. Under a D-2 student visa, students can work part-time up to 20 to 30 hours per week after their first semester, depending on their academic standing and Korean language proficiency.

### How strong is Sogang's corporate reputation in Korea?
Sogang is considered an elite institution. Major conglomerates, including Samsung, SK, and Hyundai, actively recruit Sogang graduates due to their academic discipline and analytical skills.
