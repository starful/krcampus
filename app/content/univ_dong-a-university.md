# Comprehensive Study-Abroad Guide: Dong-A University in Busan

## 1. University Overview

Dong-A University (DAU), located at 37 Nakdong-daero 550beon-gil, Saha-gu, Busan, 49315, South Korea, is a highly prestigious and historic private institution. Established in 1946 under the core values of "Freedom, Truth, and Justice," DAU has developed into a major academic hub, hosting over 1,450 international students. The university maintains an estimated acceptance rate of 65%, presenting an accessible yet academically rigorous option for driven international applicants.

A major advantage of Dong-A is its colossal network of over 200,000 alumni active across key sectors like business, engineering, and politics in South Korea, offering graduates excellent career mentorship and job placement channels. 

Structurally, DAU features three distinct specialized campuses integrated seamlessly within Busan. This design allows students to learn in highly targeted environments. Additionally, Busan provides a fantastic quality of life with affordable living costs that are substantially lower than the Seoul Metropolitan Area, making DAU an ideal destination for global scholars.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

For students seeking education without prior Korean skills, Dong-A offers the Global Business Academic Program (GBAP). Fully taught in English, GBAP is an elite track within the College of Business Administration designed to cultivate global corporate leaders. The curriculum integrates practical business training, international finance, trade, and marketing.

For Korean-medium majors, the Language Education Center provides intensive Korean courses. This pathway is highly popular due to the university’s generous TOPIK-based scholarship programs. Students can transition into undergraduate majors with heavy tuition waivers once they achieve language proficiency.

### Scholarship Opportunities
DAU offers exceptional TOPIK-based scholarships for the first semester of undergraduate study:
* **TOPIK Level 6:** 100% tuition waiver
* **TOPIK Level 5:** 70% tuition waiver
* **TOPIK Level 4:** 50% tuition waiver
* **TOPIK Level 3:** 30% tuition waiver

Subsequently, students can retain scholarships ranging from 20% to 100% by maintaining a GPA of 2.5 or higher (on a 4.5 scale).

## 5. Admissions for International Students

Dong-A University admits international students twice a year: for the Spring semester (March) and the Fall semester (September).

### Key Admission Requirements:
* **Academic Level:** High school graduation certificate or equivalent, verified via Apostille or Consular confirmation.
* **Language Standards:**
  * For Korean Tracks: Minimum TOPIK Level 3.
  * For English Track (GBAP): IELTS 5.5, TOEFL iBT 80, or equivalent.
* **Financial Sufficiency:** A bank balance certificate showing at least $16,000 USD (estimated equivalent in KRW).

The application process requires completing an online application, mailing physical credentials, and passing an interview.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

Dong-A operates three specialized campuses strategically located in Busan, South Korea's second-largest city:
* **Seunghak Campus:** Located on the slopes of Mount Seunghak, it houses engineering, health, and athletic departments with vast sports facilities.
* **Bumin Campus:** A central urban campus housing business, social sciences, and law programs, featuring a modern design and the historic university museum.
* **Gudeok Campus:** A peaceful green campus dedicated to medicine, nursing, and design, offering a classic collegiate atmosphere.

Living in Busan offers an unparalleled coastal lifestyle. From Gwangalli Beach to traditional markets, the city is dynamic and affordable. Monthly off-campus living costs average 800,000 to 1,000,000 KRW, which is roughly 40% cheaper than Seoul.

## 7. FAQ

### Is a TOPIK score mandatory for admission?
No. It is not required if you apply to the English-medium Global Business Academic Program (GBAP). However, for Korean-taught majors, a minimum of TOPIK Level 3 is required.

### Can international students work part-time in Busan?
Yes. Under the D-2 visa, students can work part-time (typically 20-30 hours per week) after completing one semester, provided they meet GPA and language requirements.

### Are dormitories guaranteed for new international students?
Yes, Dong-A prioritizes newly admitted international students for its fully equipped on-campus dormitories at both the Seunghak and Bumin campuses.

### How do living expenses in Busan compare to Seoul?
Busan is highly affordable. Off-campus housing, dining, and daily living costs are estimated to be 30% to 40% cheaper than the Seoul Metropolitan Area.

### What support services does Dong-A provide for global students?
Through the Office of International Affairs, Dong-A offers support including visa processing, pre-arrival counseling, airport pick-up, cultural field trips, and peer mentoring.
