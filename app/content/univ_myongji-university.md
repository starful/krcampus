## 1. University Overview

Myongji University (명지대학교), founded in 1948, is a prominent private university in South Korea known for its global outreach, academic excellence, and holistic student development. Guided by its values of love, truth, and service, Myongji hosts a diverse international community of roughly 2,800 international students.

With an estimated acceptance rate of 65%, the university offers an accessible yet prestigious pathway for global scholars. Myongji features a unique dual-campus system: the Seoul Campus hosts the Humanities and Social Sciences, while the Yongin Campus is dedicated to Natural Sciences, Engineering, and Arts. Through partnerships with over 300 universities in 40+ countries and industry-tailored curriculums, Myongji provides outstanding support systems and practical education, ensuring high graduate employment rates.

---

## 2. English-Taught & International Programs

Myongji University actively expands its global footprint through international study programs. Although Korean is the primary language of instruction, the university offers English-taught courses, particularly within the College of Business Administration and the College of ICT Convergence.

For students needing language preparation, the Myongji Korean Language Education Center (KLEC) provides an intensive Korean pathway. This program equips students with the TOPIK Level 3 proficiency needed for undergraduate admission. Additionally, partnerships with over 300 global institutions facilitate robust exchange programs and dual-degree pathways, letting students enrich their international perspectives.

## 3. Faculties & Academic Strengths

The academic structure of Myongji University is strategically split across its dual campuses to optimize resource allocation, research output, and state-of-the-art facilities.

| Campus | College / Faculty | Academic Focus & Strengths |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Seoul Campus** | College of Humanities, Social Sciences, Business Administration, Law | Focuses on liberal arts, global trade, administrative sciences, and law. Programs are highly integrated with metropolitan business networks. |
| **Yongin Campus** | College of Natural Sciences, Engineering, ICT Convergence, Art & Physical Education, Architecture | Concentrates on STEM fields, design, performing arts, and advanced technologies. Home to cutting-edge research centers. |

Key academic strengths include the College of Architecture, which was the first in Korea to obtain international validation from the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA). Additionally, the College of Engineering and College of ICT Convergence are highly regarded for their practical, industry-aligned curriculums that collaborate directly with leading technology firms in Korea.

## 5. Admissions for International Students

Myongji University runs two main admission cycles per year: the Spring Semester (commencing in March, with applications open from September to November) and the Fall Semester (commencing in September, with applications open from March to May).

International applicants must meet the following general requirements:
1. **Academic Records:** Graduation from an accredited high school for undergraduate programs, or a bachelor's degree for graduate programs.
2. **Language Proficiency:** TOPIK Level 3 or higher is required for Korean-taught programs. For English-taught tracks, a minimum score of TOEFL iBT 80, IELTS 5.5, or equivalent is required.
3. **Financial Capacity:** A certificate of bank balance showing at least $18,000 to $20,000 USD (or equivalent) is necessary to secure the South Korean D-2 student visa.

## 6. Campus Life & Location

Myongji’s dual campuses offer two distinct, enriching student lifestyles:
* **Seoul Campus (Seodaemun-gu):** Positioned in the dynamic heart of Seoul, this campus is close to popular student hubs like Sinchon and Hongdae. Students benefit from an energetic urban environment, countless cultural experiences, and easy access to Seoul's public transportation network.
* **Yongin Campus:** Located in a scenic green zone of the Seoul Capital Area, this campus offers a classic, spacious university atmosphere. It features advanced laboratory facilities, sports complexes, and a peaceful environment ideal for research and arts.

Both campuses feature modern, secure on-campus dormitories. The university also offers a dedicated "Global Buddy" mentorship program, international student counseling, and cultural excursions to help students adapt smoothly to life in Korea.

## 7. FAQ

### What is the estimated acceptance rate for international students?
The acceptance rate is estimated at 65%. While admissions are highly accessible, applicants must meet academic and language proficiency requirements to secure their place.

### Can I apply if I do not speak any Korean?
Yes. Students can apply to English-taught tracks or enroll in the intensive Korean Language Education Center (KLEC) at Myongji before starting their degree programs.

### Are international students allowed to work part-time?
Yes. D-2 visa holders can work part-time up to 20 hours per week during semesters, and unlimited hours during vacations, subject to university and immigration approval.

### How do I commute between the Seoul and Yongin campuses?
While faculties are separated by campus, the university runs regular shuttle buses between the two sites, making transit highly convenient.

### Does Myongji guarantee dormitory housing for international freshmen?
Myongji heavily prioritizes incoming international students for its modern on-campus dormitories, though students must apply on time during the registration window.
